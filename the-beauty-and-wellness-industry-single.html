Attract and Retain Customers with An Effective Online Presence

It’s Thursday evening and you’ve just been reminded that it’s your turn to make the group plan for your meetup with friends at the weekend.

You want to plan something fun at a place that will really wow them, so you mentally prepare a list of different ideas, from restaurants, theatres, music shows, to a spa day. What do you do next?

You reach for your phone, or your laptop, and begin browsing.

Gone are the days of flipping through a newspaper or picking up the phone to call. Customers want access and they want it now. Website browsing is the most popular way to conduct business because of its efficiency and accessibility.

"Less than 15 seconds. That’s the average time spent on a website. And that’s how long you have to capture someone’s attention." - The Daily Egg

People form an impression within 7 seconds of meeting someone new. Online it’s a little more generous at 15 seconds. That’s right, a quarter of a minute to win someone over on your landing page.

Imagine you’re accessing your company’s website for the first time. Ask yourself:

  • Is it clear what you do?
  • Is it too busy, or perhaps too bare?
  • Can you easily locate the key information a guest will need to inform their decision-making process?
  • Most importantly, is there a way for them to act (e.g., does “Book Here!” jump out in an obvious place)?

If you’re unsure about any of the answers to these questions, then this course is for you! Read on to learn about the key elements of creating an effective online presence.

Why is an Online Presence Important in the Beauty and Wellness Industry?

Websites may have once just been a digital business card but not anymore. Today, websites represent your entire brand identity. It is your business portfolio, advertising, and receptionist all rolled into one.

Therefore, the time and energy you invest in building a strong online presence can really help to propel your business to the next level. And on the other hand, it can really hinder your growth and customer acquisition if it is not representative of your brand or delivers a negative user experience.

The beauty and wellness industry is not exempt to the importance of having an online presence. In fact, because we are appointment based and sell a look, our web presence may be most important of all.

Across Zenoti customers, 29% of online booked appointments are from new guests.

Your website serves as your greatest tool for tapping into an entirely new audience base of prospective guests. Consider your experience from working at the front desk. Phone interactions with new guests typically take longer than conversations with an existing guest. This is because new visitors have questions (understandably so!) and for that reason like to access a website. A website enables them to have a browse and get a feel for the brand before they make the decision about whether it’s a good fit for them and their needs.

Once your website has caught their attention, they can take their time exploring without feeling like they are imposing on anyone or pressured to make a commitment. Unlike calling a receptionist, who may only have three minutes to book an appointment, a guest accessing your website and online booking platform has no time limit to shopping around. And that is a good thing!

Besides, do your receptionists really have time to talk on the phone with every new guest?

Your website is essentially a taste of your business, and the information on your page are your samples. They are being consumed by a lot of people, and you want to pass the test.

Creating a First Impression

The experience a guest has with your website is even more important than the experience that they have in your center. This is because your website is open to the world 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. This means it has the capability to reach more people which is a great thing for your brand awareness, but have you ever stopped to consider what first impression those visitors get when they land on your home page?

Within the first fifteen seconds the user needs to know what you do and be convinced that you’re the best at it. Whether you are trying to attract business executives, busy parents, or savvy college students, you will not get far if your website experience is poor.

If your website is frustrating, the guest is not going to chance walking through your doors. If the experience is seamless and guest friendly, they will associate your salon with being guest centric as well.

Consider what else a user may see when they enter your landing page.

  • Does your website represent guest engagement?
  • Can they see what you’ve curated on your social media?
  • If guests are asking questions or providing feedback, are you responding so that your community can see your business is responsible and provides follow-through?

Digital interactions should mirror your in-person interactions. You would never think to not call an upset guest back, so why would you leave someone hanging online?

Finally, prospective guests want to see your photo gallery. If you do not have one, that can be a huge red flag. You sell a look, whether it is clear skin, beautiful nails, or healthy hair, if you are not showcasing your photos on your website, you are not promoting what it is that you do best.

Online presence is an amazing tool, but for every reward there is a risk. The risk? If your website is not designed properly, it will drive guests away from considering you in the future.

Driving Success with Online Booking

These days, online booking is an expectation. Guests want the convenience of being able to access the scheduler anytime, anywhere with the click of a button. Without worrying if their kids are screaming, traffic noises interfering or that they are out of breath from having just got off the treadmill, like you would with a phone call. Almost half of individuals prefer to make a booking outside of business hours. After all, we all think about our to-do list when we are falling asleep don’t we?

Amongst Zenoti customers, 46% of guests prefer to book outside business hours.

What you may not realize is that online booking is also your new best receptionist. Why?

No step is ever missed, making online booking the easier, more efficient process.

Plus, online bookings has been shown to drive more revenue...

“115% increase in average booking price when treatments are booked online” (Zenoti Medspa customer).

Online booking is also proven to improve visit frequency. Amongst Zenoti customers, guests who book an appointment online visit 1 more time per year, or book 10 days sooner than guests who book via phone. One visit may not seem like a lot, but multiply that by the number of different guests that you see in a year, and you are talking about serious revenue.

Additionally, online booking significantly helps to reduce the number of no-shows that affect your business. Particularly if you request credit card information to finalize the booking. Through their partnership with Zenoti, a leading salon customer decreased no-show appointments by 35%, recapturing a little over 1/3 of their bookable appointment blocks.

BeBalanced Hormone Weight Loss Centers saw a 10% increase in overall booking efficiency with Zenoti.

A Platform for Marketing Announcements

Your website IS your marketing tool. It is a simple and effective way to showcase everything that you want your guests (current and future) to be aware of. It is your opportunity to brag about why you are the best and how the value of being a guest of yours is better than the competition.

Your website is a great place to share what is new and fresh about your business. Everything from new services, new service providers, new locations, new partnerships, and new products can be easily viewed and accessed. Keeping your guests in the loop of what is fresh within your company will keep your business new and exciting.

Your website has a lot of real estate, and that space is best utilized when it features some of your most popular promotions and campaigns.

Whether you are highlighting your loyalty program, Refer-a-Friend campaign, or gift card promotion, you are appealing to the customer’s desire to save money and feel like they are a part of something. In the eyes of your guests there is great value in these promotions.

A successful marketing announcement calls for an action. “Click here” or “buy now” buttons make it convenient for guests and generate sales for your business. Utilizing banners, side panels, and imbedded URLs ensures that the guest on your website knows there is an opportunity to maximize upon their appointment value.

One of the best advantages of marketing online is that it is more cost-effective than traditional marketing.

With traditional marketing you are paying to print and distribute marketing materials. Every time you run a new promotion, update your service menu or revamp a program you must invest in reprinting new materials. When marketing on your website it is as simple as removing one image and replacing it with another, with little turnaround time. This means you can rest easy knowing your guests have access to updated information and are aware of current promotions and offerings.

Remember, your website is accessible to anyone, at any time. Prospective guests are viewing your website and if they like what they see they can request to connect with your business. Perhaps they enter their email address to be included in future communications from your salon or spa. Voila! They are now on your mailing list, even if they have never received a service from you. By making sure that prospective guests have a way to connect with you, you capture them for the future.

Enabling E-commerce on Your Website

How many times have you shopped online in the last three months? If you’re like most Americans, it’s probably a lot.

And if you are thinking that year was an exception because of Covid-19 you would be mistaken! The number of online shoppers is expected to grow to over 278 million in 2024 (fitsmallbusiness.com).

Capitalize upon this and further enhance the guest experience by incorporating online shopping into your website.

Zenoti customers generated $1 million in online sales with the Shopify integration in the first quarter (QuickSight).

Offering your retail products online makes them accessible to millions of viewers. This means you no longer need to rely on street traffic, or service provider recommendations to move the products off your shelves. If you make your own products, it can even help to grow brand awareness.

Ultimately, online shopping is becoming part of the salon and spa ecosystem.

In today's world guests are more cognizant of their choices and aware of what they are using for their beauty and haircare routines. You may have heard a guest say, “I want to look that up” and you know that the likelihood that they will leave and return for the product is slim. By integrating e-commerce to supplement sales recapture guests that may have not been ready to make a purchase decision at the time of checkout. You can also entice them to shop with you in the future by integrating their loyalty points in with their product purchases.

Zenoti customers realize a 300% jump in both order volumes and value using the Shopify integration.

It is not only about product sales. You can also capture a piece of the digital gifting industry by making gift cards available for purchase online. Gift givers will appreciate the convenience of purchasing their gifts in the comfort of their own home - especially last-minute shoppers!

Three months post-implementation, a Zenoti customer was already driving 52% of gift cards purchases online.

We already know that in this industry guests are coming in for their visits less than five times a year. So, what happens if they need replenishment before their visit? Combat the temptation of shopping from a big box store by creating a digital shopping experience. This way you’re not only marketing to your guests when they browse your website, but you’re creating an effortless experience by allowing them to purchase with the click of a button.

Accessing Website Performance Insights

Your website is up and running! Now what?

Once you’ve launched your website, you have access to data which can help you to better understand how it is performing. You’ll have insights into most visited pages and where people are bouncing off to help you identify what should be refreshed and optimized to drive the best results.

These insights provide a heat map for you to analyze the user behavior and the performance of your site at a glance.

“Hot” areas gained the most traffic, guests either clicked, stopped scrolling, or spent an extended time on a particular page or part of your website. “Cold” areas are those where a guest spent the least amount of time, they did not effectively capture guest attention. Knowing how your website performs helps you to find answers on how to get site visitors to convert to purchasing and/or booking.

Analyzing each step of the booking process highlights where individuals are dropping off (which points to this step needing improvement) and how many users started the process compared to the number of successful bookings. With this knowledge you can continue to enhance and improve your website, and your business.

If you are choosing to feature e-commerce on your website, you can also track the performance of product sales. Are your top sellers the same as those in-salon? Do the reviews reflect the number of sales? Of guests that purchased online, how many returned to purchase a second time?

Based on the performance on the website you can determine what products guests are drawn to, even when a service provider is not there to make a recommendation. Perhaps those products deserve a spotlight in-salon?

Overall, website performance insights serve as the “fly on the wall”. You suddenly have visibility into success rates and can better determine which promotions drive the most business, which part of the booking process loses the most guests and how many of your appointments are booked online. By understanding how guests interact with your website, you can identify trends and optimize for further engagement.

Key Takeaways

Whether your guest is walking through the front doors of your salon or spa or visiting your website for the first time, they are forming a first impression of your brand. Failing to dedicate the time and energy into building a positive website experience leaves a lasting first impression - and not the good kind!

A guest decides in fifteen seconds if they are going to shop on your site, how are you going to make the most of those valuable seconds?
