Six Strategies for Boosting Customer Retention

It would be simple if business success relied solely on technical and interpersonal skills. But the fact is that many guests determine whom and when to visit based on more than skill alone.

Luckily, there are six proven strategies to improve guest retention — which go beyond technical talent.

Next, we’ll explore each of them.

1. Offer Loyalty Programs

New guests often come in with coupon codes or expecting a discount. They pay less than your loyal guest at checkout and then 7/10 times you never see them again.

And then, there are your regular guests. They follow healthy habits like purchasing retail and rebooking their appointment — but are expected to pay full price. This doesn’t leave them feeling very appreciated.

It's time to start treating your regular guests as valuable members of your salon and spa family. And a loyalty program can help you do just that.

An effective loyalty program keeps guests coming back to your salon or spa, even as you face challenges like staff churn and growing competition. Loyalty programs can be as simple or as robust as you want. A business can honor rewards based on spend amount or guest behavior, such as providing a review, rebooking, or referring a friend.

Loyalty programs are proven to keep guests engaged with your brand and your program. It’s a powerful way to avoid discounting by awarding extra points to encourage guests to try new services and products, or to book during a timeframe with lower utilization rates.

A good loyalty program requires little effort from your staff and creates an exemplary experience for your guests, helping to improve relationships and drive loyalty.

2. Promote Memberships

Amazon Prime, Costco, and Walmart are just a few big players in the membership game. They've probably already convinced you that being a member with their brand has massive perks — whether it’s free shipping, same day delivery, or discounts on gas.

Once enrolled, those businesses become top of mind for your shopping needs because there’s extra value in shopping there. For the business, there’s even more value for your enrollment.

Memberships are popular (and profitable)! Recurring membership sales improved 41% over last year, with some customers seeing a 55% growth. (Quicksight)

A spa or salon membership program is an ongoing promotion for guests.

Once a guest enrolls in your program, they receive special benefits associated with their membership. That might be discounts on services or products, or credits toward a free service.

Memberships are a powerful way for you to increase average spend, boost visit frequency, and encourage guests to try new services. They offer a simple, automated way to upsell!

“Memberships ensure we don’t lose our guests for any reason...we get a higher lifetime value from members... more than 30% of our spa and salon revenue comes from memberships. Clearly, it’s in our interest to maximize revenue from memberships.”

- Hylton Lipkin, Head of Operations at Apollo Health

When you integrate your membership program with your operating or marketing software, you can reach your guest base all at once with unique offers. You can use automation to bring guests back in and encourage more spend on each visit. When you modernize your membership program, you'll save time for your staff and see a direct result on your bottom line.

3. Launch Promotions

Let’s think about Starbucks. How many times have you been able to find a coupon before you hit the drive-through for your latte? Probably none.

Now think about a Thursday afternoon when it’s BOGO Happy Hour, or the first Friday in November when they are gifting a free holiday cup with the purchase of a seasonal drink. It’s packed with customers, with lines around the building. Those are successful promotions.

Your guests want to feel like they are getting a great deal. In fact, 39% of consumers believe direct incentives like money, discounts, or free swag can elevate the chances of referring a business. Promotions show guests you are grateful for them, and considerate of their hard-earned money.

While you may not want your brand identity to scream, “discount!” you still want to consider implementing promotions throughout the course of the year, so that your guests can shop and receive services with a little more ease (and a little less budgeting).

5. Rebooking

Imagine this: you visit a bank to ask for a loan, offering a compelling business plan that focuses on guest service, marketing, and innovation. Then, the loan officer looks at you and asks, “what guarantee do you have that your guests will return?” Without a rebooking percentage, you have nothing to offer. This metric is crucial, and it’s often overlooked.

“Rebooking” refers to the number of invoices rebooked for any future date on the same day as the service, or number of invoices serviced. So, the percentage of invoices where guests make their next booking on the same day as their last booking.

Guests may rebook at the end of their service, online when they get home, or call later that day. "Saving the date” with their guest service providers ensures future business and lessens the risk of guests bouncing to competitors.

“In 2015 the national average for rebooking was only around 10%, which means most salons don’t do it well (if at all).”  – American Salon

It is not surprising that the best time to book a guest’s next appointment is before they leave your salon or spa. Pre-booking boosts guest retention and helps you better forecast how busy your schedule will be in the coming weeks.

Have you considered how you can use pre-booking to increase guest visit frequency and revenue?

“If you’re not getting 10 rejections a day, you’re not trying hard enough.”  - Paul DiGrigoli (American Salon).

Overall, if you have reminders, incentives, and a process for pre-booking in place, every individual has the potential to become a repeat guest.

6. Service Provider Retention

It goes without saying that we wouldn’t be able to do what we do without service providers. They are the talent, and without them we do not have a business. So, how are you keeping your service providers happy at work?

The cost of losing a member of your team can add up to thousands of dollars. Even more frightening, 76% of guests confessed to feeling more loyal to their service provider than to the business, so the odds of retaining a guest if their service provider decides to leave are less than 25%.

Next time you detect a red flag for a high performing or influential team member churn risk, act fast. While it doesn’t only hurt morale in your salon, it also impacts your business.

Furthermore, it’s important to take the time to determine what your action plan will be when turnover does occur so that you can be proactive and not reactive.

Key Takeaways

Spas and salons survive on repeat business from regular guests. These guests provide predictable revenue and help you meet monthly revenue targets. And since you can’t rely on finding new guests every month, it’s important to do what it takes to hold on to the guests you have.

The cost of not retaining guests is enormous. When you lose guests, you not only lose revenue — you also increase profits for your competition.

The good news is that you can strengthen your guest retention rate by implementing the business strategies outlined in this course.
