Foundational Elements for Guest Loyalty and Retention

There’s no denying that highly skilled service providers are the foundation of the salon and spa industry. You may have the friendliest team members, a gorgeous salon, and the best technology, but if your staff can’t execute an amazing technical service, nothing else matters. That’s why a successful training program and emphasis on education is critical for your business.

What’s the next big differentiating factor between a new guest and an existing guest?

Trust.

Trust is earned when actions meet words. Your current guests are returning because you earned their trust through the service you provide and the quality of the recommendations that you give. And they demonstrate this appreciation by spending more money.

Returning guests spend 120% more than new guests each year.

How?

Because they are more likely to consider recommendations of new services and products from someone they trust. This opens the door to upgrades, add-ons, and product purchases, all of which result in dollar signs. Higher ticket sales also mean higher gratuity for the service providers, which keeps your talent happy and lowers turnover rates.

We’ve all had our bad days. Maybe a color formulation didn’t take right, or sensitive skin caused an adverse reaction during a treatment. It happens. The good news is that when those bad days hit, loyal guests are more likely than a new guest to give you another chance. They also offer valuable feedback over their lifetime visits that helps you to improve your business, address a problem before it grows, or coach a team member on their performance.

Your guests want to belong to something. They want to feel like they have finally found their beauty and wellness expert and their "place.” Offering programs and launching initiatives initiatives to keep them engaged and returning helps them to feel valued as your guest. They’re giving you their business, but what is in it for them (besides a beautiful haircut)?

When we speak about guest retention, we're referring to the percentage of guests who returned for their next service within 90 days of their latest visit. That's slightly less than the industry average, so when setting goals, we would suggest you aim higher.

Industry average visit frequency is 4.88 times per year.

If you can gain a visit, at the very least, once every three months you have established a line for a lifetime of loyalty.
