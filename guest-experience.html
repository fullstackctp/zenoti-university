Build a Strong Business Foundation with Your Guest Experience

A few decades ago, if one of your guests didn’t like the service they received or an interaction they had with your brand, you only risked them telling people in their immediate circle of influence — their family and friends. In today’s digital world, however, reviews are now a quick click away and are accessible anytime, anywhere, through multiple channels.

One critical review has the potential to reach, and turn away, dozens, if not hundreds, of potential guests. This can negatively affect your business and your brand before someone ever sets foot in your spa or salon. That’s where the guest experience comes in. Creating a strong, branded guest experience isn’t just an integral part of your business — it’s the foundation of your success. A strong guest experience supports all the key elements of your business and makes your guests feel like VIPs at every interaction.

In this course you’ll learn why a strong guest experience should be the foundation of your business and the four essential parts of business it will positively impact.

Why the Guest Experience is Important?


In the U.S. in 2014, there were 650,000 barbers, hairdressers, and cosmetologists. Schwartz Cosmetics predicts that number will increase nearly 10% by 2024. So, even if you offer the best haircuts, if the whole experience you give your guests doesn't exceed their expectations, then they may not choose you a second time.

    • In fact, according to Salesforce, “80% of customers feel the experience a company offers is as important as their products and services.”

      That’s exactly why it’s more important than ever for your guests to feel like they’ve received the best experience possible.

      But this experience isn’t just about what happens when they’re in the chair.

      A true guest experience starts with your creative marketing and personalized guest communications, builds with every intentional guest touchpoint at the appointment, and continues through instant feedback systems and attractive rewards programs.

      Essentially, the guest experience is the foundation of your business. When your business has a memorable guest experience that will directly result in:

Building Your Brand Reputation


Did you know that dissatisfied customers tell twice as many people about their negative experience than satisfied customers talk about their positive experience (as reported by Sharpencx.com)?

    • “Almost half of today’s consumers won’t consider doing business with a company that has less than a four-star rating.” ― Neil Patel, Co-Founder,Crazy Egg, Hello Bar and KISSmetrics

      The customer experience that you provide dictates your brand’s reputation. Having a bad reputation is detrimental to your business. This is because negative reviews spread twice as fast. So, even with recovery efforts, it will take twice as long to repair. Offering a five-star service coupled with a five-star guest experience is the basis of building a strong reputation and a strong business.

  • The Purpose of Valuing Your Brand Reputation

    • We already know it can be difficult to earn positive reviews. While guest feedback is an important part of your business strategy, it shouldn’t be the driving factor behind building your brand’s reputation.
    • So, what is the purpose of building a strong brand reputation?
    • It’s all about how you make your guests feel. For example, when someone carries a designer purse or drives a luxury electric vehicle, they are choosing that brand because it makes them feel a strong sense of pride whenever they use it. That’s exactly how you want your guests to feel about you.
    • When one of your guests are asked, “where do you go for your hair color?” you want them to glow with pride while they excitedly share everything about your business. You want every single guest to have confidence that they belong to a salon or spa that’s not only great at what it does, but that also gives them that VIP treatment they’re craving. Then, and only then, will your brand reputation take on a positive shape and begin to organically grow.

  • Brand Reputation + Guest Experience

    • Every business has a palpable energy that guests can almost see and feel. You can too, if you pay attention. To start thinking about what brand reputation your guest experience is building, ask yourself these questions:
    • If you walk into a business with great energy, where people are smiling and there is a strong sense of team culture, that energy is infectious and will make you feel invigorated. Plus, if you can count on that same vibe every time you visit, you’ll feel more confident recommending that brand to your friends.

    • “Just having satisfied customers isn’t good enough anymore. If you really want a booming business, you have to create raving fans.” ― Ken Blanchard, Business Consultant & Author, “The One Minute Manager”

At the end of the day, businesses dream of having a brand reputation that brings people to their stores in lines out the door. But that kind of reputation and those “raving fans” don’t just come by accident. Creating an unforgettable guest experience will go a long way in affecting how your business is perceived and in positively building your reputation as a must-visit spa or salon. How you treat your guests, and how they feel when they enter and leave your building impacts every aspect of your business. Put this way, raving fans are your livelihood.

Increasing Guest Spend


Consumers open their wallets and spend their money all the time.

But the key is in making your guests happy about spending their money at your spa or salon and choosing to come back again and again.

Guests will choose to spend money at your business if:

    1) They feel appreciated and valued by the staff and the brand.

    2) The high-quality services and products offered feel worth the money spent.

  • Harness the Power of Consultations

  • Your goal is to elevate your guest experience in a way that positively increases how much a guest will spend at their appointment. One of our biggest tips to achieve this goal?
  • Create standout consultations.
  • Yes, you heard us correctly.
  • Consider this: In most salons, the standard visit looks a lot like the guest comes in, you chat a bit, then you get to work. Right? Do you see the problem with this picture?
  • The consultation is one of the most underutilized parts of a guest’s appointment. If you intentionally take advantage of this window of time in your service provider’s first interactions with their guests, you’ll be elevating a key part of the guest experience that can generate more spend per customer.

  • “97% of stylists say that they give a consultation with every guest, every time, but 90% of guests say that they have never had a consultation with their hairdresser.” ― Barbara DeLaere, Global Brand President, Aveda

  • A proper consultation serves many purposes. It helps the service provider learn what the guest feels that they need from the appointment and then aligns those goals with the services they provide. But it also creates the perfect opportunity for service providers to make valuable recommendations for enhancements. Not only will this give your guests a better experience, but you’ll also get significantly more value from that appointment block.
  • Establish strong consultation practices that encourage your service providers to listen, empathize, ask questions and provide solutions on how their guests can achieve their hair and beauty goals. When your guests feel genuinely listened to, they will know without a doubt that you have their best interests at heart. You will have invested your time, your talent and your expertise with the guest. And in exchange, they will invest in what you believe is best for them.
  • Consider your current consultation practices: If it only includes asking questions like, “What are we doing today?” or, “Same thing as last time?” are you really meeting your guest’s needs? Remember that the purpose of the consultation is to uncover their goal during this time that they have with you. Try shifting away from generic, empty questions and engaging in open-ended conversations that open the door for dialogue. For example, a question like, “Tell me about your hair?” allows you to incorporate add-ons and product recommendations seamlessly throughout the appointment.
  • Your guests want to be more than an appointment on your books. When you intentionally build a rapport with your guests, you earn their trust, and in exchange they’re more receptive to your recommendations. Whether those recommendations are enhanced services or products for maintenance, they translate to a better experience for your guest, and add more value to the appointment. The overall guest experience you offer, including the consultation, has a direct effect on how much a guest will invest in you.

Growing Retention Rates


Retention is all about understanding how many guests are coming back to you for services, and how many visited you once but didn’t return. It’s a key business metric that’s oftentimes undervalued and overlooked when running a beauty or wellness business.

But, an increase in guest retention will directly increase the profit and productivity of your business.

And the proof is in the numbers. Salesforce reports that just “a 10% increase in customer retention levels can increase the value of your company by 30%.”

Another study found that 25% to 40% of the total revenue of “stable businesses” comes from returning customers.

There are many factors that can affect the retention rates of your business — some are within your control and some, quite frankly, aren’t. Focusing on those parts of business you can control, like the guest experience, is key to increasing your retention rates.

You may be surprised to hear that the number one reason a guest decides to leave their hairdresser is because they decided they’re ready for a change, and they don’t feel like their hairdresser is willing or able to provide that change. A service provider should never be afraid to ask a guest if they’ve thought about doing something new with their look. Asking questions is good guest service.

    • “The number one reason clients leave their hairdresser is because they feel the hairdresser cannot offer them CHANGE.”― Marco Pelusi, Celebrity Hair colorist, OwnerMarco Pelusi Hair Studio West Hollywood

In addition to opening the door to new possibilities with their look, you should also never be afraid to share your accolades, certifications and specialties with your guests. If your guests know you specialize in a particular service, you automatically add value to their appointment. They may be more likely to request those services from you, whether today or at a future appointment. For example, just because your guest may have never considered hair extensions or smoothing treatments before does not mean they won’t consider them in the future.

Does your guest know whether those are services that you offer?

Promoting guest-centric programs, like memberships and loyalty rewards, are another great way to increase retention. Guest loyalty will grow as they feel valued and are incentivized to choose your business. It’s a win-win situation!

Besides offering new ways for your guests to save money, you can also enhance guest experience and increase retention by directly inviting the guest back in to see you. Not only does this show them you enjoyed your time together and further strengthen relationships, but guests will also start looking forward to spending more time with you in the future. Just as you would put a wedding or a party in your calendar, you can save the date with your guest.

If you want to improve guest retention, then the experience that you offer needs to be prioritized just as much as the service that you provide. They could have received an amazing massage, but if they didn’t feel valued for being there, the benefits of that massage are short lived. But, once a guest feels that they are appreciated, a positive connection forms between your business and that guest. And when that connection forms it becomes less about if they will return and more about when they will return.

    • "It is not your customer’s job to remember you, it is your obligation and responsibility to make sure they don’t have the chance to forget you.”― Patricia Fripp, Award-Winning Speaker, Author & Sales Trainer

When a guest feels like their visit to your salon or spa was less about maintenance and more about an experience, they’ll be more eager to return. While there are many benefits to having loyal guests, the major takeaway is that loyal guests are the foundation of your business thriving beyond the present and into the future.

Creating Brand Advocates


Whether you use an iPhone or an Android, drink Coke or Pepsi, drive a Dodge or Chevy, you are practicing brand loyalty every time you use that company’s products. And, as a loyal member of a brand, you unconsciously spend each day championing that business.

Your business has its own set of champions — guests who receive amazing services and experiences and tell people about it. The level of training that you possess, the friendlier that you are, the more that you can surprise and delight, the more guest champions you will create. This is extremely important because your champion guests are your marketing troops, and they do some of the most valuable work for your business.

Marketing to a prospective guest can cost up to 5 times more than existing guests (Forbes). But not if you’re encouraging current guests to be your guest champions. If the service they received is top notch and the experience was superb your guests will talk about it, post about it, or leave a flattering review.

Every guest of yours is your walking billboard, which is unique to our industry. It is your responsibility to make sure that your guests are always looking their best. The guest experience doesn’t stop at the chair. To ensure that your “free billboard” always displays your best work it’s crucial to take the time to recommend products that will help them to recreate the look at home. Be sure to also rebook their next appointment before they leave so they’re coming back in at the right time to keep their look fresh.

Another, and more subtle, form of a recommendation?

Offer gift cards for your salon, spa or specific services.

Your biggest advocates are also more likely to want to share their amazing experience with people they care about. Give them the chance to do exactly that by offering gift cards for your spa or salon. This will not only help fuel your gift card programs but it will also help expand your guest base. Every appointment is an opportunity to build rapport, strengthen brand loyalty and inspire guests to become your best brand champions.

Key Takeaways


You and your service providers chose this career path because of your desire to help people look good and feel great. Creating a guest experience is about more than how your guests feel though. It’s about multiplying the positive impacts you can have on your guests’ lives and on your business’s future success.

You are just one business in a billion-dollar industry. There is competition all around you. If you want guests to choose your business over any other, you must have talent coupled with an ability to make your guests feel great during every part of their visit. When your guests feel great, they will invest in your business, keep coming back to you, recommend you to their closest friends and stand behind you and your brand. And that alone makes building an unforgettable guest experience more than worth it.
