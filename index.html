Driving growth through Gift Cards

The global gift cards market size was valued at $619.25 billion in 2019, and is projected to reach $2,076.51 billion by 2027, growing at rate of 16.2% from 2020 to 2027 based on market trends, according to a recent report by Allied Marketing Research.The culture of gifting specific items has evolved over time towards buying gift cards to give the recipient more choice and flexibility. Businesses such as Starbucks, Target, Macys, Nordstrom’s and even grocery stores have leveraged this growing trend to successfully grow their revenues, brand awareness and customer base. In the past couple of decades, the internet has made it very convenient to send gift cards electronically to loved ones. With recent travel restrictions due to Covid, we have seen this drive even greater growth in gift card sales. The increasing availability of gift cards in fully digital formats where they can be sent, tracked and used online, has driven an increase in demand among consumers, including young adults, who conduct most of their transactions online

Relevance in the Beauty and Wellness space

There is a global trend around people valuing "experiences" rather than “things”

Consumers, especially younger ones, are increasingly inclined towards experiences over material gifts.

According to a survey conducted and shared by Eventbrite

63% of US adults

(ages 18 – 65+) would prefer to receive an experience gift than a material gift during the
holiday season

3 out of 4 millenials

would rather spend their money on an experience than buy something desirable and this “experience generation” is now a third of the U.S. population.

While experience can cover a broad range of activities, beauty and wellness experiences are amongst the most meaningful experiences that can be accessed by people in a city at almost any time and within easy reach. There is tremendous and growing interest in wellness and rejuvenation to combat the stresses and impact of everyday life, and there is a very significant opportunity for salons and spas to capture this market through gift cards. When your core business is around making people look and feel good, you are one of the best places for consumers to buy a gift of experience for their loved ones.

This growing trend in gifting beauty and wellness services is also validated when we look at our customer data. We looked at our top 10 customers with a focused gift card program and found that their gift cards grew by XYZ percent over last few years, making up ABC% of their overall revenue. Clearly these gift cards are having a big impact on their overall revenue and profitability.

Gift cards can be brought to life with creative promotions and strategies that bring in new customers and make existing ones more satisfied. For example, when a salon offers a customer a bonus value or bonus gift card when they buy a gift card, they make the offer even more compelling. The gift receiver usually spends more than the value of gift card and gets exposed to more services or more high-end services, which makes it more likely that you will retain them. That makes the discount worthwhile. Such creative strategies have worked very well for our key customers and made gift cards a particularly important strategic tool for beauty and wellness businesses.

Common Gift Card Trends and Strategies in the Industry

When used to their full potential, gift cards can significantly increase profits and draw new customers to your center. How? By turning existing customers into brand loyalists and expanding the market through their influence

1. Include Digital Format as well, not just Physical gift cards.

Attractiveness of physical gift cards – packaging.

Make gift cards easy to purchase and consume by offering both physical and digital formats. Digital gift cards are a very significant portion of sales of gif cards overall. 52% of gift cards purchased online (3-month average post implementation) at one of our top customers.

Major restaurant chains, such as Starbucks, Chipotle, and Chili’s, and many beauty and wellness establishments offer customers both digital and physical gift cards. These cards make it convenient for customers to pay for their services using an app or in-establishment card reader to speed up the checkout process.

Young users are particularly attracted to digital gift cards. 55% of young adults say that they are very comfortable giving and receiving gift cards from brands they like.

Online gift cards can be more easily personalized and customized to suit the gifting occasion, including deciding the precise amount or service or product that can be gifted. This has been instrumental in making gift cards much more versatile and popular than traditional gifts.
