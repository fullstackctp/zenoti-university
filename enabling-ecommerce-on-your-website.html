Enabling Ecommerce on Your Website

How many times have you shopped online in the last three months? If you’re like most Americans, it’s probably a lot.

In 2020 there were more than 255 million digital buyers. That’s 88% of the U.S. population (Statista). And if you’re thinking that year was an exception because of Covid-19 you would be mistaken as the number of online shoppers is expected to grow to over 278 million in 2024 (fitsmallbusiness.com)

Capitalize upon this and further enhance the guest experience by incorporating online shopping into your website.

Zenoti customers generated $1 million in online sales with the Shopify integration in the first quarter (QuickSight).

Offering your retail products online makes them accessible to millions of viewers. This means you no longer need to rely on street traffic, or service provider recommendations to move the products off your shelves. If you make your own products, it can even help to grow brand awareness.

Ultimately, online shopping is becoming part of the salon and spa ecosystem.

In today's world guests are more cognizant of their choices and aware of what they are using for their beauty and haircare routines. You may have heard a guest say, “I want to look that up” and you know that the likelihood that they will leave and return for the product is slim. By integrating Ecommerce to supplement sales in your salon and spa, you can recapture guests that may have not been ready to make a purchase decision at the time of checkout. You can also entice them to shop with you in the future by integrating their loyalty points in with their product purchases.

Zenoti customers realize a 300% jump in both order volumes and value using the Shopify integration.

It is not only about product sales. You can also capture a piece of the digital gifting industry by making gift cards available for purchase online. Gift givers will appreciate the convenience of purchasing their gifts in the comfort of their own home - especially last-minute shoppers!

Three months post-implementation, a Zenoti customer was already driving 52% of gift cards purchases online.

We already know that in this industry guests are coming in for their visits less than five times a year. So, what happens if they need replenishment before their visit? Combat the temptation of shopping from a big box store by creating a digital shopping experience. This way you’re not only marketing to your guests when they browse your website, but you’re creating an effortless experience by allowing them to purchase with the click of a button.
