Providing a Seamless Customer Journey

Introduction

Now, more than ever, time is the most precious form of currency. It seems the moment one thing comes off our to-do lists, three things are added. Luckily, today’s technology compresses the time it takes to do most things. What once took hours can now take minutes, and what used to take minutes can now be done in mere seconds!

Because of their demanding lives, guests want convenience and ease. They expect hassle-free interactions and a quick, seamless flow. Simple tasks like booking an appointment, checking the date of their last service, or confirming the balance on a gift card, are all things guests want to do on a “self-serve” basis. With digital advancements, your business can – and must – deliver those self-service tools.

There’s an obvious upside for guests when they can take matters into their own hands, but this also presents you and your staff with many advantages. Rather than focus on their “transactional” needs, you can put your efforts into delighting your guests during the time they spend with you.

In this lesson, we will study the customer journey, from a guest’s first booking to their ongoing engagement, highlighting along the way how well-integrated technology can further enhance their experience.

What you’ll learn:

  • How to engage with a customer even before they book or step into your shop.
  • Why online booking is the preferred way to schedule an appointment.
  • How to empower clients with a guest-centric – yet technology driven – visit to your business.
  • How to positively interact with your guest as they reflect on the time spent in your salon, spa, or barber shop.

Did you know?

  • 73% of consumers say a good customer experience influences their brand loyalty.
  • 63% of consumers report the best brands exceed their customer journey expectations.
  • 65% of consumers feel a positive customer experience is more influential than ads or marketing.

Meet Lily

To show how seamless the customer journey can be with intuitive and well-integrated technology, we’re going follow a prospective guest named Lily.

Lily just moved into a new home in a new town. She and her family are finally unpacked and settled in, and now Lily is starting to look for service providers in her area. Her kids need a new pediatrician, her husband is looking for a new massage therapist, and Lily wants to find a really great salon. Let’s follow Lily on the journey from her living room sofa to the salon of her dreams.

Click an image to start Lily’s journey.

Lily is winding down from the day. Her kids are in bed, the dishes are done, and she’s finally relaxing on the couch. Surfing her cellphone, Lily thinks about how badly she needs a haircut, but she’s new to the area and needs to find a good salon.

Lily goes to the search engine on her mobile device and types “haircut near me” in the search bar. A list of ten local salons pops up. In an instant, Lily can see if the salons are close to home and how many stars they’ve received.

Lily clicks on the first option and decides it’s not for her. The second option is even less attractive. The third time was close, but the fourth one was the winner. What factors led to such a variety of first impressions? Read on to learn why the fourth brand captured her attention.

Click (+) to see Lily’s impression of each website.

Salon Website #1

When Lily selected the first option from the search results, her first impression was that the website looked nice, but the only option to book an appointment was to call the business. Since it was late in the day, Lily could not call, and she thought, “I want to get something on my calendar now, so I don’t have to think about it again.”

Salon Website #2

When clicking the second option on the list, Lily was totally turned off. The graphics were horrible, and she noticed spelling errors on the homepage. She immediately clicked back to the search results and thought to herself, “Ugh, that did not look very professional.”

Salon Website #3

Lily clicks the third listing and it’s instantly more promising than the last. There is a link to download a Service Menu PDF, so Lily can review their offerings and starting prices. “This could be a winner,” she thinks to herself as she clicks around to look for images of their work. Unfortunately, there are no pictures and no links to Instagram or Facebook. Leary of how talented the service providers may be, she moves on to the fourth search result.

Salon Website #4

Finally, Lily’s first impression is positive, and she thinks, “This website is professional, and it looks like a cool place.” The page was bright and crisp, the photos were high-quality, and there was a “Book Now” button at the top of the page. Lily was also impressed to see that the salon had their own mobile app. This is just what Lily was looking for, so she clicks on “Book Now” and begins the booking process. “This is a good experience; I’m excited I found this place!” she thinks to herself.

Engage Review:

  • Make it easy for customers to do business with you: Link your social media, email address, and phone number to every presence you have online so a guest can quickly and easily find your shop or connect with you.
  • Communicate with customers using their preferred mode: Use text messaging to communicate with your guests. 70% of customers will choose a “message us” button over calling your business.
  • Don’t let prospective guests fall through the cracks: Create a sales opportunity for any phone inquiry, email inquiry, or walk-in guest. Management can follow-up and track these leads to measure success with conversion.

BOOK

Click an image to learn about Lily’s booking experience.

As Lily starts booking, she’s prompted to create an account with her name, email, and mobile number.

Then, in a few clicks, she selects a “Haircut” with “Any” of the displayed stylists on Thursday at 6:30 PM.

When confirming the booking, she’s asked to enter her credit card number. Lily was surprised at first, but then read the number is only used to reserve the booking.

Moments later, Lily receives a thank you text asking her to confirm her appointment.

Lily responds “Yes” and then adds the appointment to her Google calendar from a link in the text.

Another link allows her to download the salon’s mobile app.

“That was easy,” she says to herself, and she jumps over to social media to wind down for the night.

The next day, Lily is watching a soccer game when she thinks, “I really need an eyebrow wax.” She texts the number that sent the confirmation message, “Can I book an eyebrow wax after my haircut on Thursday?”

Within moments she receives a reply, “Yes! We can add a brow wax. Is there a service provider you prefer?” Lily responds, “Anyone works. Thanks!” She then receives a text that her wax has been booked.

Here’s how the salon followed up with Lily:

Reminder: When Thursday morning rolls around, Lily receives a reminder text for her haircut and brow wax. She confirms the time is correct, and that both the haircut and eyebrow wax are listed, and responds, “I’m looking forward to it!”

Digital Guest Form: After responding to the reminder, Lily receives a link to the salon’s Guest Intake Form. She appreciates being able to complete the basic questions about her health history and medication list from the convenience of her phone. In no time she submits the form.

Book Review:

Lily’s journey shows not only how important it is to offer online booking to your guests, but also that it’s a vital touchpoint of your guest’s journey. Lily has never stepped into the salon, she wasn’t referred by a friend, and she does not have a new guest promotion, but the salon’s online presence and ability to book online, along with connecting with the staff via text, has made a personal impact on Lily.

  • It is quickly becoming a preferred method for booking. Data shows that over 70% of a salon’s customers prefer to self-book online over other channels.
  • Your appointment book becomes available to guests 24/7. On average, Zenoti customers see 46% of self-service bookings taking place outside business hours (9pm-8am) via website or mobile app.
  • 29% of new guests are booking their appointment online. These new guests can book quickly or take their time to browse and explore everything that you have to offer.
  • 77% of consumers have a more positive perception of a company when they offer a texting service.

VISIT

The day has arrived! Lily sets out for the salon.

Click an image to learn about Lily’s visit to the salon.

It’s finally Thursday, and Lily uses her navigation to find the salon.

When she pulls in the parking lot, she gets an alert on her phone, “Hi Lily! Would you like to check in for your appointment at 6:30pm?”

“Well, that’s cool,” Lily thinks to herself as she clicks the big “Yes” button.

“You’re checked-in for your appointment. We will let you know once your service provider is ready.” Lily sits in her car and checks email until she receives a text that her stylist is ready.

Lily enters the salon for the first time and is greeted by a smiling face. “Welcome, you must be Lily, my name is Chloe, and I will be your stylist today!”

At her station, Chloe first consults with Lily, and then follows with a shampoo and incredible long-layered haircut. Lily feels as great as she looks.

Chloe recaps the appointment and recommends the smoothing serum she used. Lily is hesitant to purchase because she has a cabinet full of products. To her delight, Chloe understands and adds the recommendation to Lily’s notes, in case she’s interested next time.

Chloe uses her employee app to alert the service provider who will perform Lily’s brow wax.

“Hi Lily, my name is Andrea, and I will be providing your spa service. Let me show you to our spa.”

In the treatment room, Andrea thanks Lily for completing the Guest Intake Form in advance. “During my review, I did not find any contraindications, so we’re all set to get started.”

Andrea waxes Lily’s eyebrows as they chat briefly about Lily being new to town.

Here’s how the check-out experience went for Lily:

Touchless: At the conclusion of the appointment, Andrea asks Lily if she’d like to check-out at the front desk or if she’d prefer to use her mobile device. Surprised, Lily responds, “If I could use my phone, that would be great.” Andrea explains the salon has technology at every stage to provide convenience and keep guests safe by encouraging a touchless environment.

Check-Out: From the mobile app, Lily can access the appointment and click “Check-Out”. Once she is on the check-out screen, Lily can add a tip and enter her payment details. The app shares a confirmation message that her check-out is complete, and she receives a copy of the receipt in her email.

Self-Pay: Lily walks through the reception area on her way back to the parking lot. She notices that the two receptionists at the front desk have a line of guests waiting to check out. On top of that, it sounds like two phone lines are ringing. “Self-pay was the way to go,” Lily thinks to herself as she smiles and waves goodbye to the employees behind the desk.

Visit Review:

For Lily, the in-salon experience was greatly enhanced with the use of Zenoti Go. Her connections with the stylist and esthetician were focused on the services they provided, not on check-in tasks, filling forms, or waiting to pay.

  • No more waiting at the front desk to check-in. Zenoti Go uses geofencing to recognize a customer’s arrival and sends them an auto-prompt to check-in on their mobile device. Once check-in is confirmed, the service provider receives a notification on their personal device.
  • After the service, your customers breeze through the exit, skipping the check-out line. Your provider uses their mobile device to add any retail purchases or add-ons to the invoice and initiates an automatic payment.
  • Customers receive an Auto-Pay confirmation on their app and can add additional tips on the go. Customers can also add tips and directly pay on the app. With alerts on payment failures, salon staff can be assured that every departing customer has paid.

REFLECT

After an appointment concludes is an important time of reflection for guests. You will want to encourage guests to take action on the following right away – ideally on the same day of the appointment they just had.

Click each arrow below to see how Lily reflected on her visit:

As Lily walks to her car, she receives a notification asking her to rate her experience and provide feedback. Lily gives five stars and comments that both Chloe and Andrea provided wonderful services and made her feel very comfortable and welcomed.

While Chloe was cutting Lily’s hair, she shared that to keep her style looking its best, she should have it trimmed every 8 weeks. Lily did a quick schedule check and was able to rebook with Chloe while still in the chair, saving Lily from having to think about it later.

Lily loves the experience at her salon. She’s a member of their loyalty program and gets points for the services she receives and the products she purchases. Lily uses the salon’s mobile app to track the points she earns and redeems.

Lily happily refers her friends and family to the salon. Using the app, she can send them her unique link for booking an appointment. Once her referrals go to the salon and complete a service, Lily earns even more loyalty points.

Reflect Review:

Technology keeps you and your guests connected even after their appointment is over.

  • Rating their experiences and sharing valuable feedback is super quick for guests with an intuitive, tag-based format. Tracking and follow up is easy using a guest satisfaction dashboard. You can coach your providers based on feedback, follow up on negative experiences, and win back unhappy clients. You can also build your brand’s online reputation by redirecting customers to share online reviews.
  • Rebooking is easy with past and future appointment tracking available in online and mobile booking. In no time, guests can rebook their requested service with their favorite provider on their ideal date/time.
  • Happy customers come back and refer new customers. Integrated technology lets them refer-a-friend digitally by sending emails, texts, or social media links to book with your business. Your guests will be notified when a friend or family members finishes a service and points are awarded. Guests will be further invested with your loyalty program and drive additional visits as they watch their points accumulate.

ENGAGE

The salon delivered an exceptional guest experience to Lily. From the search that began in her living room, to booking online, to a quick and touchless check-out process, and all the way to post appointment reflections, Lily is delighted. But, without consistent engagement from the salon, she still may not become a loyal and retained client.

Here’s how the salon can stay engaged with Lily:

Follow Up: To kick-off engagement after the visit, the salon can follow-up with styling recommendations and retail items they can use at home, so that the impact of the service that was delivered lasts longer.

Communicate: Staying top of mind for a new guest is easy when you send special announcements or newsletters filled with tips and information. These touchpoints show that you appreciate the guest and want to provide them with value even when in-between visits.

Offer: Another way to created a continuous engagement loop is to reach out with the right offer at the right time to the right client. For example, create a marketing campaign that targets guests who had color six weeks ago, but do not have a future appointment. A discount or promotion may be just what they need to book!

Key Takeaways

You have many opportunities to win a new guest, from the second they find your business online or hear a rave review from someone they trust. Every touchpoint a guest has with your business can either strengthen or weaken their perception of your brand. Your goal is to have your guest thinking:

  • “It is so easy to get an appointment here.”
  • “I’m able to do everything at my own pace when I use my phone during my visit with their business”.

When your guest experience allows you to connect with customers whether they are at home, on the job, or in your shop, they feel as though they belong to something. They are part of your family, and they can keep in touch in the palm of their hands the way that they would with their own friends and family. You connect with them on an emotional level, and you stay connected with them even when they are away.

Why focus on guest experience touchpoints? To power an exceptional, touchless client experience. For the ease and simplicity. And to keep salons always open, and always connected. There is not enough time in the day, by investing in ways to optimize your processes, and give back some of the guest's time, your guest will continue to invest in you. Afterall, time is the most precious form of currency.
