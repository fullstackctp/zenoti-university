Grow Your Business with Memberships and Packages

These days, most of us belong to something. Whether you're enrolled in Amazon Prime, Walmart+, Birchbox, or any other brand, you’ll more than likely have a membership that you use.

In exchange for signing up, you receive more value for shopping at a place that you’ve always loved. Maybe it was the free shipping, same day delivery, discounts on gas or just the ability to shop there that got your attention—the bottom line is that you got some amazing perks out of it.

The beauty and wellness industry is no different.

We also have guests who are looking to add more value to their experience with us. We get that it can be risky to send out coupons for $20 off or hook a guest by promising 20% off their total service. But converting a visit into membership? Now that has benefits for both the business and the guest.

Did you know? The industry average for customer retention is only 23%.

What’s the best perk to memberships and packages?

They get guests to come back to your business!

There’s more in it for guests because of the investment that they’ve made to be a part of your program. And by giving them a reason to come back—other than your amazing service, of course—you're more likely to retain them.

The more a guest visits your business, the more loyal they’ll become. Your name becomes synonymous with the service that you offer. Establishing loyal guests is every business owner's dream because loyalty correlates with average spend—not to mention helps drive new business through referrals.

So, in this course, we'll explore two different strategies that salons and spas can leverage to achieve the same goal: increase retention and build customer loyalty.

Let’s dive in!

What is a Membership Program?

Let’s start with the basics.

Put simply, it’s an ongoing promotion for customers. Once a guest enrolls in your membership program, they receive special perks through it, like discounts on services and products, or credits toward free services.

Membership programs can be structured in a variety of ways, but typically you’ll want the customer to pay a membership fee when they sign on. A basic membership plan can offer a set of services plus a balance of credits for a cost lower than the total value.

36% of Zenoti’s top 50 U.S. customers (spas, salons and medspas) are currently offering a membership program.

Membership programs provide significant value to your salon or spa business. Implementing one can help you retain your customers and keep them coming back for more. They increase brand loyalty, strengthen connection and encourage guests to try new products and services they wouldn’t have considered, resulting in more frequent upselling and cross-selling opportunities.

Returning customers spend 120% more than new customers per year

Memberships are not only popular, but they are profitable too. Recurring membership sales improved 41% year-over-year, with some customers seeing a 55% growth (QuickSight). The effects of memberships have also aided COVID-19 relief efforts, as businesses are recovering by driving recurring membership sales, which are 41% higher over the previous year (QuickSight).

What are the types of memberships?

When it comes to memberships, there are two different types.

  • Recurring memberships that are billed on an annual basis or through a monthly fee.
  • Nonrecurring memberships that are made through a one-time fee.

Benefits of Offering Memberships

To your business:

Memberships offer businesses no shortage of benefits. Here are a few of them:

  • Capture guest loyalty

    • When your guest has a membership with your business, they’re less likely to bounce to another competitor that’s offering a deal. The value that they feel from the perks earned and the experience you provide makes them one of your brand’s greatest ambassadors.

  • Increase average customer spend

    • Members are more likely to purchase gift cards, buy retail and try upgraded services than non-members.

  • Generate predictable revenue

    • Since guests who have memberships are more likely to come back for services, memberships can play a big part in growing your business through continuous and predictable revenue.

  • Conversion to other service categories

    • Depending on the terms of your membership program, memberships can be a fantastic bridge to another department in your business. For example, a spa membership allows perks on both facials and massages, which has the power to benefit two different business units.

  • Retail conversion

    • Customizing your membership to include discounts on retail can help to convert spa visits to retail purchases and increase total sales in product purchases.

  • Team member retention

    • Guests become loyal to the program, and not the provider. Team members know that if they consider leaving your business, they are at more risk of their guest not following them.

To your team:

Memberships also offer a wealth of benefits to your team as they:

  • Increase frequency of visit

    • Once a guest has invested in your program, they tend to feel obligated to come in to reap the benefits. Who wouldn’t want to maximize their membership and hard-earned dollars? More visits equate to higher utilized service providers. And as we all know, the busier the provider, the happier the provider.

  • Boost average ticket

    • You can set exclusions when publishing your program. Many companies may not include their introductory and standard treatments in order to drive business to the more enhanced services with the bigger ticket price. Even when sold at a discounted price, high-end services have a higher average ticket than classic, resulting in more revenue for the service providers.

  • Happier service providers

    • If you’ve successfully increased the frequency of visits, your providers will be busier and earning more as a result. As a bonus, they’ll also see an increase in gratuity as their guests come in more often and are calculating their gratuity from the original price (according to Daniel Post, spokesperson for the Emily Post Institute of Etiquette, spa guests should be tipping 20% of the original cost, not discounted price).

To your guest:

  • Discount on services

    • With memberships, guests can receive great services for a discounted price. Plus, there’s less risk in trying new services, since they are not paying full value.

  • Service credits

    • Give customers the flexibility to convert service credits to dollar credits and use it however they’d like. Clients can use service credits to redeem for other services. For example, they can use two credits of a 30-min service to redeem a single 60-min service..

  • Product savings

    • Guests can enjoy your retail offerings at a discounted rate, loosening their budget on items that your retail shop carries.

Packages 101

We all need maintenance when it comes to our health and beauty. And while selling one service is a small success, selling eight or ten at a time is really something to celebrate. It means your guest likes the service you provide, and they’re planning to come back a handful of times, at the very least.

And that’s where packages come into play.

First of all, what exactly is a package?

A package is a group of services and products sold as a collection, usually at a lower rate than the sum of the individual items. Businesses can choose to group services based on a complementary set of items, best-selling services, popular gifts and treatment plans.

24% of Zenoti’s top U.S. customers (spas, salons and medspas) are currently offering a package/series program.

Packages are a terrific way to upsell to your customer and ensure future visits for your business. For example, if a guest comes in for a facial, the receptionist could inform them that a package is available that includes the facial plus a manicure for only a little more than the cost of just the facial. Odds are the guest will most likely try the package.

Promotional packages can also result in a guest spending less than what they would have otherwise spent. A guest who regularly comes in for a facial, pedicure and manicure would spend less than they expected by taking advantage of the package.

Benefits of offering a package:

As with memberships, there are a wide variety of perks to offering your guests packages—for your business, your team, and your guests.

To your business:

  • Increased guest loyalty, since customers invest in future services with you.
  • It’s easy to drive revenue with unique offers (for instance, by providing special services only through packages).
  • Increased cash flow.
  • A steady revenue stream.

To your team:

  • Increased frequency of visit—which means that service providers are more likely to be busy on any given day.
  • Alleviates congestion at the front desk, since invoices can be closed with one click.

To your guest:

  • Seamless checkout, since you can automatically apply benefits and discounts for package holders.
  • Easy decision making and ability to gift an experience with a package gift card.
  • Receive benefits on services they love.

Things to keep in mind

What do you do before bringing in a new product line? Your research, of course! Offering packages is no different.

While offering packages has been proven as successful, you may want to look at other considerations before you pull the trigger. In our industry, package sales seem to take a backseat to membership sales. Whether you’re considering packages as a single offer or in conjunction with a membership program, you should be aware of the following:

  • More liability: guests pre-purchased services and may be less understanding to booking obstacles such as high traffic.

  • More inclined for refunds, if services have not been rendered.

  • May restrict guests from trying other services in the department, if their package benefits do not support new services.

What Are the Types of Packages?

  • Day Package: A group of services that can be redeemed in a single visit (e.g., a spa day or bridal package)
  • Series Regular Package: A fixed number of benefits spread over time (e.g., a 10 massage detox package)
  • Series Offer Package: A “Buy X, Get Y” type of offer on services (e.g., Buy 5 massages, get 2 free)
  • Series Custom Package: Template based on where quantity of benefits can be customized (e.g., Weight loss packages with custom quantity of sittings for different guests)

Eight Steps for Creating a Successful Program

Now that you understand the types of memberships and packages, and can see what other businesses have adapted, you’re all set to create your own program!

According to SpaBusinessEducation.com, there are eight necessary steps when it comes to creating a program. At each one, you should ask yourself a series of questions so that you can be proactive and avoid any delays at the time of launch. We’ve outlined these below.

  • Design the program
  • Name it! Choose something that’s on brand with your organization.
  • Set the price
  • How much will you sell it for, and what will the incentive be?
  • Tip: Consider how many services you would need to provide at the discounted rate to generate your current revenue. Is that number of services feasible, or a stretch? If it doesn’t seem realistic, you should reconsider the discount.
  • The industry average for savings offered is 10-20% (Prosolutionssoftware.com).
  • Manage memberships and packages
  • Create the membership or package in your POS System.
  • Develop an FAQ for any questions that may arise.
  • Collect payment
  • Are you requiring your guests to pay upfront? Will you be adding a card to their file for automatic, recurring payments?
  • Are they glowing and relaxed?
  • Can customers purchase online?
  • Set guidelines
  • Will you exclude any services from the offering?
  • Are benefits limited to certain business hours (days and times)?
  • Are benefits transferrable to other guests?
  • Are benefits available at any location (for multi-center businesses)?
  • Is there an expiration date? Does validity begin from purchase date or first redemption?
  • Marketing
  • Draft your membership agreement.
  • How will guests know that this is available?
  • How can you target new guests who are looking for a great deal?
  • Team training
  • Does your team understand how the program works? Can they articulate the exclusions?
  • Do you have their buy-in? Have you properly explained the value?
  • Tip: Set performance expectations. You may even want to implement rewards to ensure a successful launch.
  • Track member usage
  • How will you keep your team engaged with enrollment numbers?
  • How will you notify a guest that their program is expiring?
  • What metrics will you use to analyze the success of the program?

Key Takeaways

Your guests want more than a perfect foil or nail design. They want to belong to something and feel valued. And they want to feel like they can stretch their dollar further at your business, because you care about them.

While memberships and packages both help to boost guest loyalty and retention rates, they should be recognized as two vastly different programs. In fact, they’re so different that many businesses offer both programs for their guests.

So, whether you're opting for a membership program, a package or series program, or both, we encourage you to use these eight steps and best practices as a guide for creating what works for your business.
