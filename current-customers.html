Marketing to Current Customers

The original 80/20 rule has nothing to do with the ratio of veggies to carbs on your plate. It’s been shown that upwards of 80% of a company’s revenue comes from only 20% of its customers. Just like broccoli and green veggies are an important part of a balanced diet, retaining existing clients has significant impacts on the health of your business.

Current customers are important to your business because they:

  • Typically spend more, 67% more to be exact
  • Consider recommendations of new products or services
  • Give you a second chance if they experience any issues
  • Refer family and friends, referred clients have 16% higher lifetime value than a client who wasn’t referred
  • Provide valuable feedback that helps you strengthen your business
  • Review, review, review! These are the people you want to invite to leave reviews and recommend your services to friends wherever possible. You can do this by incentivizing them or simply asking.

Why email campaigns? Because they drive results.

This is where you can realize your full marketing potential. Email is referred to as pull not push marketing. Meaning that your email subscribers have come to you and are wanting to hear from you compared to you sharing your message out and hoping they will hear it.

Like a text from a good friend, you can provide more value to your clients with targeted emails, sent to segmented lists, which allows you to get ultra-personal and connect with your guests on another level.
