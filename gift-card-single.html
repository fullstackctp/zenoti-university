Grow Your Customer Base and Increase Revenue with Gift Cards

Your location is open, you have the best providers in town, the salon or spa experience you offer is like no other, and you only provide the highest quality service. What’s next? Gift cards.

Did you know the global gift card market was valued at $619.25 billion in 2019? And it’s projected to reach $2,076.51 billion by 2027? Growing at rate of 16.2% from 2020 to 2027, according to a recent report by Allied Marketing Research, gift cards aren’t just nice to have, they’re a requirement. Businesses such as Starbucks, Target, Macys, Nordstrom Nordstrom and even grocery stores have leveraged this growing trend to successfully grow their revenue, brand awareness and customer base.

COVID-19 restrictions have further contributed to the rise of digital gift cards with more individuals identifying them as a convenient gift-giving solution when you can’t shop or visit someone in person. With over 85% of adults shopping online, it’s time to meet your guests where they’re at and provide them with what they want by offering a gift card program. In this course you’ll learn why your salon or spa needs to sell gift cards, and how to do it.

How Gift Cards Can Help Your Business

Gift cards can significantly increase revenue and bring new guests through your doors.

How?

By turning existing guests into brand evangelists and expanding your audience through their influence. But it’s about more than simply having gift cards available. It’s about creating an experience. That’s why we’re sharing examples of creative ways to expand your gift card horizons, motivate purchases, and make gift cards an important tool in the ongoing success of your business.

Let’s consider a few scenarios where creative use of gift cards can drive success...

Scenario 1

A salon offers a Buy One Get One (BOGO) spring promotion for its gift card program. When a guest buys a gift card for $100, they get a bonus value of $20 added to it.

Jane, a loyal customer is motivated by the special offer and the beautiful packaging that she sees at the salon and decides to buy her friend, Carmen, a salon gift card for her birthday.

Jane goes to Carmen’s birthday party with a beautiful gift bag from the salon and presents it to her friend. People notice the brand and develop an interest in it. Carmen has never been to this salon before and now she’s excited to go!

11% of costumers aged 18 to 34 are more likely to visit a store they otherwise wouldn’t have because they’ve received a gift card.

Carmen makes an appointment at the salon the following week. She enjoys the visit and books a subsequent appointment.

Over the course of the two visits, she samples a service she wouldn’t have otherwise tried (for example, a balayage in addition to a haircut) and spends $200 ($120 gift card value and an additional $80 to get two great services, instead of just a haircut). She is happy with the experience and becomes a loyal customer.

In general, 65% of customers spend close to 40% more than the value of the card they received.

Overall, the gift card program and a small promotional investment of $20 has helped the salon expand its brand awareness, gain a happy new customer, and earn back much more in revenue than the original investment.

Scenario 2

A new office complex has opened nearby containing many small businesses, such as a physician’s office, dental office, law firm, and accounting firm. The salon manager, Katie, promotes gift cards with the business owners and offers a bulk purchase discount.

Two of the businesses decide to purchase gift cards as holiday gifts for their employees.

Now all these employees are aware of the salon and can try its services. If they have a total of 50 employees and just 20% of them like the experience, the proximity and convenience will likely convert those 10 people into regular guests.

Overall, the initial bulk buy purchase discount offered by Katie pays off in the long-term by helping the salon to capture new market opportunities and grow awareness. This results in significant return on investment if just some of this audience becomes regular guests.

Scenario 3

Use gift cards to create positive outcomes in difficult situations.

For example, Steve visits your spa for a massage treatment and is left feeling unsatisfied with the experience. Steve visits the front desk and asks for his money back. Instead of the refund, the front desk team offer him a gift card to return for a future massage for free.

Overall, giving Steve a gift card in this scenario keeps the money in the spa and offers another chance to wow him and win him back.

Scenario 4

A guest, Felicity, wants to purchase a gift to give to a loved one. Felicity knows that your salon is currently closed due to a COVID lockdown but chooses to buy a gift card so that the recipient has something to look forward to once businesses can reopen.

Overall, this helps you to make up lost revenues and enables Felicity to still support your salon or spa.

How to Create a Successful Gift Card Program

Let’s review the most important elements and aspects to consider when building out your gift card program.

Decide on Your Format - Physical or Digital

Gift cards can be made available in both physical and digital formats, and most savvy businesses leverage both options to maximize appeal to different types of customers and to suit different gift giving occasions. Major chains such as Starbucks, Chipotle, and Chili’s, and most major beauty and wellness establishments offer customers both digital and physical gift cards.

Physical Gift Cards

The right visuals are everything. Attractive gift cards placed at the checkout counter can encourage purchases. Some salons go a step further and offer beautiful envelopes and bags to carry the gift card, along with information or promotional offers. These gift packages, which often carry the salon or spa name and logo can serve as great marketing tools, especially when these gifts are given at an event.

Digital Gift Cards

On the other hand, digital gift cards offer convenience and have become a very significant portion of sales of gift cards overall. One of Zenoti’s top customers implemented a digital gift card program and found that within 3 months, 52% of gift cards were being purchased online.

Millennials are particularly attracted to digital gift cards, with 55% interested in giving one, and 67% interested in receiving one.

Digital gift cards are not only easy to buy from anywhere on your computer or phone, but they also make it convenient for the recipient to pay for their services using an app or card reader to speed up the checkout process.

Online gift cards can also be personalized and customized in more ways than a physical gift card, including gifting a service or product vs. a dollar amount. This customization is one of the reasons digital gift cards are more versatile (more on that below).

Customize Your Cards

Set yourself up for success and allow customization in your gift cards. Some guests want to share a favorite service with friends and family, while others want to gift a dollar amount. Providing flexibility in the type of gift offered will drive more sales. Allowing the gift card buyer to customize the gift card adds a further purchase incentive as they can still add personalization for the recipient.

If you know that your friend loves massages, gifting them a massage at your favorite spa (and maybe even going with them) makes it much more personal than receiving a monetary gift card. Being able to add a personalized design or message on a digital gift card also makes the experience more satisfying for the gift giver who may miss being able to deliver it in person.

Make Them Easy to Purchase

Put Gift Cards Front-and-Center Online: Gift cards should be easy to purchase from your mobile app or webstore. If customers must hunt for it on your website, they may give up before they find it. On the other hand, if someone is visiting your website and they see gift cards placed front and enter, they may be inspired to buy one even if that was not the original reason for their visit. Zenoti customer, EvelineCharles Salon and Spa proudly displays gift cards as a “Shop Online” dropdown directly on their homepage.

Encourage Gift Card Purchases at the Front Desk : Create a beautiful card rack at the front desk and let the visual appeal do the selling for you. Remember, these are gifts so offer sleek envelopes or attractive boxes to house the gift card and remove the packaging step from your busy customer’s to-do list. And remember to keep the display and packaging on brand. Every element of your business should reflect the brand logo and color scheme wherever possible.

Motivate Your Staff

Get your staff on board with your gift card program by involving them! That can look like:

  • Establish Metrics: Share clear sales goals for the program so employees know what to work toward.
  • Set Up for Success with Training: help them gain confidence about the new program and ensure they understand exactly how the program works, including what the cards can and can’t be used for.
  • Show Employees What’s in it for Them: how do staff benefit when customers purchase and redeem gift cards? This includes personal benefit (such as a friendly sales competition), or team benefit in higher revenue, more customers, and a stronger business.
