Introduction to Key Business Metrics

Introduction

The saying goes, “What gets measured, gets managed,” but with all the responsibilities of owning or managing a salon, spa, or barber shop, how do you measure what’s really contributing to the success of your business?

That’s where business metrics come into play. Zenoti has a set of dashboards that report various important industry metrics. They provide you with the insights necessary to track trends and see the progress toward – or away – from your goals and benchmarks. These metrics help you gauge how your business is performing today and what the projections are for future performance.

What you’ll learn:

  • Why these metrics matter, what they measure, what they tell you, and how you can track them.
  • How to understand the drivers of top-line, guest satisfaction, provider performance, and other operational areas.

Three reasons why business metrics matter

To really use these metrics as the business drivers that they are, it’s essential to understand WHY they matter. Let’s take a look at three reasons.

They monitor the health of your business and help you measure the vital signs of your operations.

They measure progress and help you stay on track, allowing you to identify problems or opportunities and to put the necessary adjustments into place.

They help you spot trends and analyze patterns to manage things like seasonal slowdowns, employee performance, and inventory trends.

Collections Metrics

Among the long list of metrics that are on your radar screen, sales and collections are likely the business metrics you are most tuned into. In Zenoti, sales and collections are defined in the following ways

  1. Sales are reported for closed invoices, and the sales amount excludes any applicable taxes. Sales data is available in Zenoti’s core financial reporting.
  2. Collections are reported for open or closed invoices, and the collections amount will include applicable taxes. For this lesson, we’ll focus on collections metrics, which are included in the Snapshot of your Business Metrics dashboard.

The top-line section of the dashboard is where you can find your Collections metrics and trends, as shown below.

As you can imagine, collections from services will be the bulk of your revenue. The more services you sell, the more successful your team can be. But your business growth is about more than just services. Having collection insights for each category shown below will help you analyze these important business drivers.

Service sales are your bread and butter. If your service collections are trending down, you may want to analyze your service mix. For example, if you’re selling more single process color services than highlights, your average spend will be lower. Taking this data to your colorists and coaching them on how to promote multi-dimensional color services to their guests will not only have a positive impact on your collections, but also on their paycheck.

Product sales reports on the retail items you’ve sold. While retail sales can boost your daily collections, they also allow you to analyze your product assortment. Insights from this business metric help you understand:

  • How product brands are selling
  • How individual products are selling
  • How product sales by service providers are trending
  • How successful promotions or campaigns have been

With these insights, you can assess your offerings and customize marketing strategies to help grow product sales. Using these metrics can also support your inventory processes, so your best-selling items are always in stock, and your slow movers can be discounted or discontinued.

Gift card sales include when a gift card is purchased in-store, on the web, or in the customer mobile app. Gift card sales are a vital component of your collections, as they:

  • Forecast future business
  • Promote brand awareness
  • Act as a gateway to new guests > 70% of gift card recipients are new customers to a beauty and wellness brand
  • Drive revenue growth > 65% of gift card recipients spend 40% more than the initial value of the gift card

Having a pulse on your gift card sales will help you run successful promotions. For example, during the holiday season your dashboard will show your daily gift card sales and how the current year is trending over the previous year.

  • If trending behind, you may opt to launch a marketing campaign to target guests that have purchased in the past.
  • If trending above, you may want to reward team members who have contributed the most.

For more information on how gift card sales can drive business growth, please see Gain Customers and Grow Revenue with Gift Cards.

Membership collections are reported when a guest purchases a membership plan. Payment can either be collected up front or on a recurring schedule (e.g., monthly payments). Members receive discounts and benefits which drive frequency of visits. Membership plans can entice guests to experience upgraded or enhanced services, with the intention they will return for these services in the future.

Knowing your membership metrics, you can:

  • Coach service providers that are trending behind in membership sales and help the team see the benefits for the business, the guest, and their personal growth and goals.
  • Create campaigns to encourage your guests to enroll. Or, if membership sales are down from the prior year, review your process for soon-to-expire memberships, so those sales can be captured again.

Package collections reflect the sale of a day package, custom package, or series package. Package sales provide a healthy cashflow and act as an indicator to future committed sales. Like a membership, guests can choose to purchase a package upfront or commit to scheduled payments. If you are trending behind with your package sales it may be time to invest in some marketing, service provider training, or front desk training.

Guest Retention Metrics

When it comes to measuring the success of your business, you cannot overlook the importance of guest retention. Cultivating loyal guests helps increase collections, as Zenoti data shows 77% of revenue comes from guests who have visited more than four times in a year. It’s a numbers game. The more guests you retain, the more collections you earn.

Guest retention is measured by the percentage of new and repeat guests that had a service and are booked to return within a certain timeframe (typically 90 days).

You can measure the success of your retention efforts by taking a closer look at the following metrics:

Click each category below to learn more.

Existing Guest Visits

These are the guests you’ve seen before and would like to keep coming back. This metric is valuable as it gives you insights on how your current visit count is trending.

In the Your Guest Snapshot section of the dashboard, you see how your center is performing with respect to visits by existing guests and new guests.

Rebooking

Rebooking is a crucial as it’s a leading indicator of future business and a key factor in building guest retention rates. If your guest booked a future appointment on the day of their visit, they have been rebooked.

In the Your Guests Snapshot section, you see how your center is performing with respect to visits by existing guests and new guests. On the dashboard, you see the percentage of invoices in which guests made their next booking on the same day as their last appointment. To qualify, they can rebook at the end of their service, rebook online when they get home, or give you a call later that day.

If this business metric is trending down, the best way to increase it is to have your service providers offer to rebook guests at the conclusion of their service, while they are still in the chair or treatment room. They can guide guests to the customer mobile app, or (when permissions allow) they can rebook their guest on their own device using the Zenoti mobile app.

But it’s not just up to the service provider, your business needs to create a culture of rebooking, where every guest gets encouraged to rebook after every appointment.

Customer Base Expansion Metrics

If you want to grow your business, you need to grow your customer base. Because of natural guest attrition, you’ll need to have various strategies for customer acquisition. The flip side of acquisition is retention. The average retention rate for salons and spas is 30%, so for every 100 new guests you acquire, on average, 70 of them will not return. High-performing salons and spas will set a higher benchmark for this metric, as they know letting 7 out of 10 guests slip through their fingers is not a sustainable way to grow their customer base.

New Guest Visits

Every spa, salon, or barbershop needs a constant flow of new clients if they are going thrive, let alone survive. The snapshot dashboard helps you stay on top of this important metric by tracking your New Guest Visits.

New Guest Visits is measured as the number of visits for guests who do not have a prior ‘visit’ on any previous day.

Knowing the business metric for new guest visits, which is shown in the Your Guests Snapshot section, is vital for understanding your business growth.

For example, if you are averaging 50 new guests a month, you can expect a total of 600 new guests over the course of a year. Knowing the average rate of retention is 30%, you can calculate your projected growth to be 180 new guests. That number can then be compared to previous years to determine if your customer base is growing, stagnant, or decreasing.

Once you know how many new guests are visiting, you can establish an action plan based on your needs. Whether the count is up or down, you need a plan in place.

Referral Program

After making a push for new guests and having solid retention strategies in place, you will want to have a defined referral program up and running. A referral program is a great way to empower your existing clientele to help you expand your customer base. It makes sense that your loyal guests would have family and friends that are also aligned with your brand values and offerings.

As a customer base expansion tactic, a referral program can be both rewarding for your guests and affordable for you. Even when the acquisition costs include a reward for the referring guest and their referred friend, it will likely be much less expensive than the cost of other marketing efforts.

The Zenoti Referral Reward Program allows your business to leverage this key strategy for growing your clientele, making it easy for your existing guests to share a referral code with friends and family using a text message. For more details, go to "Referral Reward Program in a Nutshell" in the Zenoti Help Center.

4 WAYS TO EXPAND YOUR CUSTOMER BASE

  1. Invest in acquisition marketing efforts or increase those currently in place. Consider the many ways you promote your business, including email, social media, and text campaigns.
  2. Assess any new guest promotions or offers you are running, e.g., have a “new guest” welcome gift or offer a free service upgrade for their next visit.
  3. Make a lasting impression by sending a personal follow-up after a first-time visit. This note can be handwritten or sent via email, but no matter the format, the sentiment will be well-received by your new guest.
  4. Create an initiative to boost your referral program or award additional loyalty points to guests who refer a friend for their first visit.

Revenue Enhancement Metrics

Once you’ve done the work to get guests in your shop and have delivered exceptional guest experience to retain them, you can build on that trusted relationship by recommending service enhancements and retail products. Doing this adds value to their experience, can extend their service results, and strengthens their trust in you. All of which can lead to an increase in their spend with your business.

Average spend is calculated as the day’s final invoice total (includes discounts/excludes taxes) divided by the number of visits.

Increasing Average Spend

It’s no secret beauty and wellness businesses are always looking to increase revenue. And a surefire way to do that is to increase your average spend. Also known as average ticket, increasing the amount your guests spend at each visit or over a set course of time (e.g., yearly) is a growth metric you will want to closely monitor and track.

REASONS TO PUSH FOR INCREASED AVERAGE SPEND

An increase in average spend per visit means your guests had higher invoice totals for any given day compared to the prior reporting period.Increasing the average spend is not only a fantastic way to drive sales, it also strengthens the guest experience and increases brand loyalty.

Product to Service Ratio

An obvious way to increase average spend is to sell retail products. The percentage of guests that purchase retail product along with paying for a service is known as the product to service ratio.

Product to Service ratio is calculated as the number of closed invoices with at least one service and one product divided by the number of closed invoices with at least one service (excludes refunds).

In the Your Locations Snapshot section of the dashboard, the product to service ratio for each of your centers is listed. Tracking this business metric on a regular basis will help you meet and exceed your average spend benchmarks.

While being obvious, it’s not always easy for your staff to increase this ratio. This may be due to a lack of product knowledge or being uncomfortable in a sales role, but consistently attaching products to your services is a major win for your business.

OVERCOMING SALES HESITATION

  • The way to overcome sales hesitation is education. To do this, ensure your service providers have initial and ongoing product training. It’s also important that your front desk staff can identify product features and benefits.
  • With a solid understanding of your product mix, your providers can “tell” rather than “sell”. Educating clients about the products they should be using to maintain their style or results should automatically be part of the conversation they have during their time with a guest.

Utilization Metrics

Another key metric for managing future success is how well your business optimizes its provider utilization. Nobody wants empty appointment books. It’s unmotivating for the service providers and bad for business. So this business metric should be monitored closely.

To maximize revenue, your employees should be providing services during all their available hours. Granted, a 100% utilization rate may not be realistic, but high performing salons and spas commit to getting as close as possible to a perfect percentage.

Utilization is calculated as service hours + recovery hours + paid block hours divided by scheduled hours during the selected time period.

This important business metric is traked in the Your Providers Snapshot section of the dashboard, where you can see utilization percentages for the selected timeframe and for the next two weeks.

  • Current Provider Utilization is the displayed first in this section. It shows utilization for the reporting time period and should be assessed regularly to help you optimize your percentage.
  • Upcoming Rates of Utilization is the second metric shown. This forecasting helps you look ahead and make the adjustments necessary to increase the rate.

*For an in-depth look at this metric, you can click Learn More to launch the Provider Utilization dashboard.

Monitoring your utilization percentage can help you properly manage your schedule. Mainly, it helps you track the number of hours that are booked with appointments and also understand how many are still available for booking.

To combat low service provider utilization, you can increase your efforts on rebooking. Successfully rebooking leads to future scheduled appointments and higher utilization.

Options to try when utilization is low for the next two weeks include:

  • Launching a marketing campaign to boost last minute bookings.
  • Adjusting the number of providers you have scheduled to avoid having technicians on the floor without clients.

Keep in mind, frequent changes to your regular schedule can lead to employee discontent. The better option is to optimize your marketing efforts and build a culture of rebooking.

Key Takeaways:

Without visibility into your key business metrics, you are driving blind. With regular monitoring of your business metrics, you know where you’ve been, where you’re currently situated, and where your business is headed.

But simply knowing your metrics and their measurements is not enough, it’s what you do with the knowledge that makes all the difference. Setting goals and benchmarks for these metrics will help you determine if you are headed in the right direction, or if you need to implement strategies and tactics to get back on course.

With the dashboards available in Zenoti Analytics Express, your key business metrics are automatically tracked and are easily accessible. With the ability to filter data in many ways, you can look at what’s meaningful to your business at the time it matters most.
