Leverage Loyalty Plans for Guest Retention

When guests consistently choose your business over a competitor that offers similar services or products, they are loyal to your brand. Their purchase decision is less influenced by price or availability but instead from relationships they have built or positive experiences they have had with your salon or spa previously.

Therefore, if a customer is loyal, they aren’t easily influenced to switch to a new brand based on short-term benefits. Instead, they value the quality and familiarity of your brand, which they are already established with. It's important not to underestimate the value of this loyalty.

Loyalty programs have evolved significantly over the last decade, with the advent of new technologies, and now come in a number of different styles and formats. From points for dollar spend, to referral rewards, and social media shares, there’s a loyalty program to suit every need and business goal. Ultimately, the continued and growing popularity of loyalty programs across multiple industries indicates its high business value.

Why is Customer Loyalty Important?

Loyal customers drive the majority of the revenues and income for a service business.

Let’s take Starbucks as an example:

Starbucks’ rewards program is often regarded as one of the best retail loyalty programs in existence and it’s easy to see why! Through it, Starbucks has created a loyal base of over 16 million customers in the US who generate an incredible 40% of its total sales in the area.

How?

Starbucks’ app has strengthened its relationships with customers by empowering them to order and pay before arrival and earn rewards such as free food, drinks, and merchandise. Customers in the rewards program also benefit from perks like free birthday drinks, access to special events, knowledge on upcoming product releases, and the ability to send gift cards through the app.

Interested to see how it applies to your industry?

Our data shows that over 77% of a spa or salon’s revenue is from customers who have visited 4-12 times in a year.

  • About 33% of the guests visited 4 or more times a year
  • They accounted for 77% of revenues in a given year

Our data also shows that unsurprisingly they spend 6-7 times as much in a year as the guests who visit 3 or fewer times, and even on a per visit basis they tend to spend more than average.

Our data clearly displays the business value that loyal customers have. Not only are they coming back time and time again but they actually spend more once there. This ties back to our earlier point about loyal customers being more receptive to product recommendations and upsells.

How Exactly Does a Loyalty Program Help?

In today’s digital world you are continuously competing for your customer’s attention and loyalty. With data and segmentation technologies, your competitors are able to target your customer base with ads across their social media and search channels at any time of the day. A loyalty program encourages customers to ignore these temptations by showing them how valued they are at your brand.

We're all human and naturally enjoy a little recognition every now and then. If a customer comes back to your salon over and over, they’ll undoubtedly appreciate a little thank you or reward. Recognizing these customers may be easy if you are a small single center but as your brand grows and you hire more team members this can get tricky.

Hence, having a clearly defined program which gives out loyalty points towards products or services is a simple, yet highly effective way of showing this appreciation. The whole purpose of this model is to reward your best customers - after all the data shows these are most profitable.

Beyond just thanking customers for their loyalty, the program does serve other purposes. You are also able to collect information on their preferences, likes and dislikes, and personal information which helps you to build a tailored, personalized experience.

Now, when the triggers for them to leave do occur – like a new salon in the neighborhood or the departure of their favorite employee– you don’t immediately lose them. These customers will continue to be loyal to your brand since they are incentivized to continue earning and redeeming points.

How Does a Loyalty Program Influence Customer Behavior?

A loyalty program can also be beneficial for encouraging guests to engage in behaviors that will further help your brand while making them feel valued and rewarded.

Some examples include:

  • Giving points if they book an appointment during off peak hours or quiet days. For example, if Tuesdays are your least profitable day, and you often have unfilled appointments, then you could extend your loyalty program so that guests earn extra points if they book an appointment on this day. This form of loyalty program helps you to optimize your resource utilization and increase revenue.
  • Providing guests with points in exchange for feedback. This not only indicates to guests that you are listening to them, and their opinion matters, but gives you the opportunity to address any negative feedback and make improvements to avoid repeat instances. In this example you would avoid losing this customer and receive important learnings for the future.
  • Encourage online bookings in exchange for loyalty points. This is a particularly beneficial strategy if you are looking to lighten the load or cut costs at the front desk. Plus, data shows that guests who engage with your business online and pay digitally spend more and visit more regularly – win-win.
  • Exchange points for certain retail products that aren’t selling well. This strategy avoids lost revenue and builds customer loyalty as the guests get a good deal on the products.

Here Are Some Creative Ways to Engage Guests in Your Loyalty Program

Examples of Successful Salon & Spa Loyalty Programs

1. One flat annual fee – unlimited benefits & perks.

This type of model encourages guests to choose you as their one stop shop by enrolling them into a program where they receive exclusive member benefits. Guests feel more value when they shop with you and will choose your business over others because of the investment that they have made.

Here is an example:

  • Enroll for $30.00 (one time-annual fee).
  • Receive free shipping on all your orders.
  • 5 loyalty points earned for every dollar spent.
  • Loyalty points are converted to salon rewards -for every 100 loyalty points earned, receive $5 in salon rewards cash.
  • Receive a birthday gift - on special occasions like your first order and your birthday, you earn double points per dollar.

2. Earn more perks when you spend more.

Encourage your guests to come in and spend money that will translate into loyalty points. You can earn rewards faster with this program than many others.

Here is an example:

  • Earn a point per dollar spent.
  • Redeem points for service add-ons, or toward service upgrades.
  • Double points for product purchases.
  • 20 points for appointments booked online.
  • 250 points for referring a new member.

3. Monthly membership to build loyalty.

Monthly memberships support frequency of visit, retention, and brand loyalty. This type of model also provides consistent monthly cash flow into your business.

Here is an example:

  • $50.00 monthly fee.
  • Membership benefits include a free service (amongst a select set), every 30 days.
  • Product discounts for members.
  • No-risk rollover options.

Key Takeaways

When it comes to building a loyalty program, creativity and patience are the key to success. Don’t expect to see results overnight but, if implemented correctly, a loyalty program can be a true game changer for your business by increasing retention rates, boosting profits, and driving guest behavior.
