How to Create a Successful Gift Card Program

Let’s review the most important elements and aspects to consider when building out your gift card program.

1. Decide on Your Format - Physical or Digital

Gift cards can be made available in both physical and digital formats, and most savvy businesses leverage both options to maximize appeal to different types of customers and to suit different gift giving occasions. Major restaurant chains such as Starbucks, Chipotle, and Chili’s, and most major beauty and wellness establishments offer customers both digital and physical gift cards.

Some salons go a step further and offer beautiful envelopes and bags to carry the gift card, along with information or promotional offers. On the other hand, digital gift cards offer convenience and have become a very significant portion of sales of gift cards overall. One of Zenoti’s top customers implemented a digital gift card program and found that within 3 months, 52% of gift cards were being purchased online.

Millennials are particularly attracted to digital gift cards, with 55% interested in giving one, and 67% interested in receiving one.

Digital gift cards are not only easy to buy from anywhere on your computer or phone, but they also make it convenient for the recipient to pay for their services using an app or card reader to speed up the checkout process.

Online gift cards can also be personalized and customized in more ways than a physical gift card, including gifting a service or product vs. a dollar amount. This customization is one of the reasons digital gift cards more versatile (more on that below).

2. Customize Your Cards

Smart businesses allow customization in their gift cards. Some guests want to share a favorite service with friends and family, while others want to gift a dollar amount. Providing flexibility in the type of gift offered will enable your business to see more sales. Allowing the gift card buyer to customize the gift card will also allow them to personalize it for their recipient.

If you know that your friend loves pedicures, offering them a gift of a spa pedicure at your favorite spa (and maybe even going with them!) will make it much more personal than receiving a monetary gift card. Being able to add a personalized design or message on a digital gift card will also make the experience more satisfying for the gift giver who may miss being able to deliver it in person.

3. Make Them Easy to Purchase

Gift Cards Front-and-Center Online : Gift cards should be easy to purchase from your mobile app or webstore. If customers must hunt for it on your website, they may give up before they find it. On the other hand, if someone is visiting your website and they see gift cards placed front and center, they may be inspired to buy one even if they were not planning to. Holiday Party Tip : Go the extra step and hold a holiday “gifting party.” Invite customers (and their friends) to shrink their holiday shopping list by stocking up on gift certificates for friends and family at your center—and make it a party. Wine them, dine them, and sell those cards.

Leverage Guest Data: Other popular gift giving occasions involve days that are personal and special to the recipient – these include birthdays and wedding anniversaries. Businesses that can leverage their customer information to create personalized offers for these occasions can increase their gift card sales

Go Beyond the Holiday Season : You can also get creative and promote gift giving for a variety of other events and even based on the seasons. Here are some seasonal promos you might consider :

  • Spring Promo- Get a fresh haircut, manicure, etc. to be ready for the season.
  • Summer Fun/Memorial Day Promo- Get a new hair style or a spa day to celebrate the summer.
  • Fall/Back to School Promo- Invest in some self-care before the start of the school year.

5. Motivate Your Staff

Get your staff on board with your gift card program by evolving them! That can look like:

  • Establish Metrics:Share clear sales goals for the program so employees know what to work toward.
  • Set Up for Success with Training: Help them gain confidence about the new program and ensure they understand exactly how the program works, including what the cards can and can’t be used for.
  • Show Employees What’s in it for Them: How do staff benefit when customers purchase and redeem gift cards? This includes personal benefit (such as a friendly sales competition), or team benefit in higher revenue, more customers, and a stronger business.

Key Takeaways

If you’re intentional with your program and stay on top of your data, gift cards can play a significant role in increasing your revenue, boosting brand recognition and expanding your customer base.
