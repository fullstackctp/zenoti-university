Setting Up Key Social Media Platforms

Setting your business up on social media can be a daunting task, with so many platforms and steps to take, it’s easy to get caught up in the weeds. Zenoti is here to help you get your socials up and running so that you can focus your time on your customers and providing that top notch salon and spa experience.

64% of people aged 18-64 are members of an online community, with a further 74% expecting their engagement to increase in the future. We know the benefits of holistic approaches to our health and wellness, and this is a valuable way to look at social media too. Zenoti wants to assist you in creating an integrated and well-balanced social media presence that helps you perform at your best online.

Therefore, we have chosen to focus on the four largest and most prominent platforms, particularly within the wellness space, to make this course as effective as possible. There have been an estimated 5.26 billion conversations or engagements regarding wellness on social media, and this trend shows no signs of stopping - it’s time to tap into it.

Instagram

Getting Started

With over 61% of social media posts discussing wellness being visual, Instagram is a key platform to utilize, and leads the pack in terms of the volume of health and wellness focused posts.

Best Practices

  • Include convenient call to action buttons, which visitors and followers can tap to make contact or do business with you. These can be added to your profile.
  • Post content, particularly Stories, often - 2-3 x per week is a great place to start, just make sure all content is on brand and relevant to your audience.
  • Feed content should look curated and be aesthetically pleasing both as standalone content, and when seen together on the grid (your profile).
  • Check your DMs regularly for any customer inquiries or to collaborate with relevant influencers.
  • Respond to any questions or comments on your feed promptly.
  • Create a branded or personalized hashtag (#) - for example BeautyFix Med Spa (shown below) uses #GetYourBeautyFix and encourages anyone talking about their salon to use this # so that they can track it and see relevant posts.
  • Utilize the Stories option to show more live and ‘in the moment’ style video and image content (more on this in our Social Media Content Creation and Curation module!). These Stories can be highlighted and categorized on your profile to make it easy to see different aspects of your offering. For example, The NOW massage boutique has used a branded cover on each:
  • Your Insights page, which you can access through your profile, offers a detailed view of how content is performing. Insights for Stories are available for up to 14 days after Stories appear. This can help you better understand if your content is reaching the right audience and how they are engaging.
  • Build up your Instagram following and community by following relevant accounts. For example, other medspas or salons, or the people who are following them, and beauty and wellness influencers that align with your brand.

Profile Set Up

Facebook and Instagram are both owned by the same company, and therefore can be linked and integrated to make managing your social media easier. Instagram is best, and most commonly, used on mobile, so the following steps are intended to be completed on mobile as well.

  1. Download the Instagram App

    The app is available for free from the App Store or Google Play Store.

  2. Set Up or Create a New Account

    Use your business email address to set up your account. Alternatively, you can select “Login with Facebook’ to link your Instagram account from the start.

  3. Create a Username

    Create a username that is either the name of your business, or an identifiable phrase that would be used to search for your business. This should be the same or standardized across platforms.

  4. Profile Picture

    Similar to Facebook, Instagram crops your profile picture into a circle. It is recommended to use your logo as your profile picture, or a photo that includes your brand name. The dimensions for Instagram are 320 x 320 pixels.

  5. About/Information

    Instagram profiles include a short bio section (150 words or less) where you can add information about your business and what you do. This should be very short and snappy, and where appropriate use emojis to break up text.

    See examples from The NOW massage boutique and Urban Glow Hair Salon:

  6. Links

    Add a link to your website, a direct booking link, or any online location you wish your Instagram audience to visit. You can change this at any time if you have any campaigns running where you want to link to a different location.

Facebook

Getting Started

Setting up a Facebook Page for your brand or business is an important step in establishing an online presence. Only a few years ago, people predominantly connected with their local business in physical, face-to-face locations, nowadays over 1.6 billion people use Facebook to connect to small businesses. Utilizing this online platform helps brands establish and maintain connections, within their surrounding communities, and beyond.

Access the link to set up a Facebook Business Page here.

Best Practices

  • As soon as your Facebook Business Page is set up, post content that is engaging and informative. Use photos and videos to liven it up and maintain an active presence, posting 1-2 x per week.
  • You can repost Instagram Stories to Facebook too.
  • Respond to any Facebook messages or comments as promptly and professionally as possible - checking this three times a day (morning, lunchtime, before end of business) will ensure you keep on top of it.
  • Join relevant industry groups to see what people are talking about. There are Facebook Groups for just about anything, so search your specialty and location and find a good fit for your business.
  • Depending on what kind of products or services your business has, you can utilize special features that Facebook offers. For example, there are options to include a menu or services list where you could showcase a spa offering.
  • Click on the Insights tab at the top of your Facebook Page to see how your audience is engaging. This will show you how many people you are reaching, and which content is resonating best. This step is imperative if you want to learn what works and continually improve your social content strategy.

Profile Set Up

  1. Naming Your Page

    Your Facebook Business Page should be named after your brand or an identifiable name that people would use to search for your business online.

  2. Contact Information

    Ensure all your contact information is up to date so that your audience can connect with you through and outside of Facebook. Setting up an additional email to handle any social media enquiries may also be useful to better manage your business’ digital presence.

    See an example from Chatters Salons:

  3. About Section

    The About section is where you give a bit of background to your business and explain what you do, and why. You can have some fun with this section and let your brand tone of voice shine through. It’s good to keep it short and sweet so that it’s digestible but informative.

    See an example from Chatters Salons:

  4. Profile and Cover Photos

    Choose photos that represent your business and brand. Your profile photo could be a logo, or something that is visible when placed in a small circle. Your cover photo can be an image of your business, products, services, or marketing material.

    Profile Photo dimensions:

    Displays at 170 x 170 pixels on your page on computers, 128 x 128 pixels on smartphones, and 36 x 36 pixels on most mobile phones. Keep in mind this will be cropped circular. Typically, a 1:1 aspect ratio works best.

    Cover Photo dimensions:

    Displays at 820 pixels wide x 312 pixels tall on your page on computers and 640 pixels wide x 360 pixels tall on smartphones. Must be at least 400 pixels wide and 150 pixels tall. This will show smaller on mobile, so do not have any crucial elements close to the top or bottom borders.

    You can also upload video files and create a cover video, so if you have high quality footage this is a great option to make your page stand out.

    Example of profile and cover photos from Chatters Salons:

  5. Call to Action Button

    As you can see in the image above, Chatters added a ‘Book Now’ button on their page that drives traffic straight through to a booking portal for their salons. You can select different button actions depending on what you want your visitors to do. For example, you can link directly to a website or add a phone button so that your page visitors are more inclined to call your business. If you are offering services, ‘Book Now’ is the best button to use, but if you do not have online booking ‘Contact Us’ is the next best option.

Pinterest

Getting Started

Pinterest is a visual platform that allows users to gather inspiration on subjects that they are interested in and collect them in specialized Boards. 60% of Pinterest users are women, which makes it an engaging place for spas, salons and medspas who cater predominantly to women as well. Despite being slightly smaller than the other social media platforms, Pinterest users are three times more likely to click through to a company’s website than any other social platform.

Best Practices

  • Create relevant Boards (no more than 10) that highlight your services and tap into industry trends (we will dive deeper into this in our Social Media Content Creation and Curation module).
  • Follow influencers and other industry leaders to see emerging trends within your field.
  • Utilize Analytics to view impressions, engagements, clicks, views and so much more. You can also select specific date ranges and view all your pins or only the pins saved from your website.
  • Use Pinterest’s Trend Page to stay up to date with popular topics.

TikTok

Getting Started

As mentioned in Module 1, TikTok is the up-and-coming short video platform that is heating up faster than the best curling iron. TikTok beauty content that goes viral has been generating serious sales for brands. For example, Deciem’s The Ordinary recently saw a 426% sales spike in its AHA 30% + BHA 2% Peeling Solution after the product caught on among the platform’s acne-positive teens.

Best Practices

  • Be more relaxed and fun when using TikTok, short, lively videos are the best way to utilize this platform.
  • Use hashtag challenges to encourage people to engage – sharing a video of you undertaking it at the salon and including the campaign hashtag will attract more viewers to your page.
  • Branded lenses come in all shapes and sizes – TikTok lets you create your own face filters and objects, allowing potential clients to have fun with them, while at the same time improving their bond with your brand.
  • Record salon processes, whether it be a before and after skin treatment, or a fun new hairstyle, this type of content will resonate well on TikTok.

Profile Set Up

  1. Sign Up for TikTok

    Once you’ve downloaded the TikTok app, you’ll be prompted to either login or create an account. You can choose to sign up using your email or phone number, in addition to a few other ways.

  2. Create a Username

    Choose a username that directly identifies your brand and ideally matches your other social handles.

    Right after this step, you will also be directed to create a password. Make sure to give the appropriate team members access to this so everyone stays on the same page!

  3. Profile Picture

    Set a profile picture that will make your brand easy to recognize, including the logo or brand name.

  4. About/Description

    Make sure you include your company’s website in the bio. You have 140 characters max to describe your business, make it fun and relatable. You can also share your other social handles in this bio.
