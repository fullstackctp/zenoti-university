Set Up Your Social Media Platforms

With so many platforms to choose from, it’s not easy to find the right ones for your business.

If you’re feeling stuck on which platforms to use, if you have signed up for them all but haven’t spent the time building out your profiles because there’s too many, or if you have your profiles set up and are ready to optimize them, you’ve come to the right place.

Zenoti is here to help you get set up on the right platform, at the right time, to reach the right audience.

There has been an estimated 5.26 billion conversations about wellness on social media, and this trend shows no signs of stopping - it’s time to tap into it.

Instagram

Why Use Instagram?

With over 61% of social media posts discussing wellness being visual, Instagram leads the pack in terms of the volume of health and wellness focused posts.

Profile Set Up

Facebook and Instagram are both owned by the same company, and therefore can be linked and integrated to make managing your social media easier. Instagram is best, and most commonly, used on mobile, so the following steps are intended to be completed on mobile.

  1. Download the Instagram app.
  2. Set up or create a new account.
  3. Create a username that includes your business name.
  4. Add a profile name that helps people discover your account. This can be your business name or industry.
  5. Add a profile photo that is or includes your logo. The dimensions for an Instagram profile photo are 320 x 320 pixels.
  6. Add your bio. The bio is 150 words or less and is where you add information about who you are what you do.

Tip: Keep your bio short and snappy, and where appropriate use emojis to break up text.

In addition to these steps, you also want to include a call to action (CTA) within your profile. This is what visitors and followers can tap to contact your business, such as a link to your website or booking page. You can change this anytime. For example, if you have any campaigns running where you want to link to a specific location or landing page.

Feed Set Up

Post content, particularly Stories, often - 2-3 x per week is a great place to start. Make sure all content is on brand and relevant to your audience. Your feed content should look curated and be aesthetically pleasing both as standalone content, and when seen together on the grid (your profile).

Create a branded or personalized hashtag (#) - for example BeautyFix Med Spa (shown below) uses #GetYourBeautyFix and encourages anyone talking about their salon to use this # so that they can track it and see relevant posts.

Utilize the Stories option to show more live and ‘in the moment’ style video and image content (more on this in our Social Media Content Creation and Curation module!). These Stories can be highlighted and categorized on your profile to make it easy to see different aspects of your offering.

Use your Insights page, which you can access through your profile, offers a detailed view of how content is performing. Insights for Stories are available for up to 14 days after Stories appear. This can help you better understand if your content is reaching the right audience and how they are engaging.

Community Set Up

Build up your Instagram following and community by following relevant accounts. For example, other medspas or salons, the people who follow them, and beauty and wellness influencers that align with your brand.

Check your inbox regularly for any customer inquiries, or to collaborate with relevant influencers. Be sure to respond to any questions or comments on your feed promptly for maximum engagement.

Facebook

Why Use Facebook?

64% of people aged 18-64 are members of an online community, with a further 74% expecting their engagement to increase in the future.

Setting up a Facebook Page for your brand or business is an important step in establishing an online presence. Only a few years ago, people predominantly connected with their local business in physical, face-to-face locations, nowadays over 1.6 billion people use Facebook to connect to small businesses. Utilizing this online platform helps brands establish and maintain connections within their surrounding communities, and beyond.

Profile Set Up

Access the link to set up a Facebook Business Page here. Then follow these steps to set up your profile:

  • Name your page. Your Facebook Business Page should be named after your brand or an identifiable name that people would use to search for your business online.
  • Add your contact information. Ensure all your contact information is up to date so that your audience can connect with you through and outside of Facebook. Setting up an additional email to handle any social media inquiries may also be helpful to manage your business’ digital presence better.
  • Create your “About” section. The About section is where you give background on your business, explain what you do, and why. You can have some fun with this section and let your brand tone of voice shine through.
  • Add a profile and cover photo. Choose photos that represent your business and brand and include your logo so you can be easily identified. The dimensions for a Facebook profile photo display at 170 x 170 pixels on computers, 128 x 128 pixels on smartphones, and 36 x 36 pixels on most mobile phones and will be cropped circular. Typically, a 1:1 aspect ratio works best. Cover photos display at 820 pixels x 312 pixels on computers and 640 pixels x 360 pixels on smartphones.
  • Add a CTA. You can select different button actions depending on what you want your visitors to do. For example, you can link directly to a website or add a phone button so that your page visitors are more inclined to call your business. If you are offering services, ‘Book Now’ is the best button to use, but if you do not have online booking ‘Contact Us’ is the next best option.

Feed Set Up

Post content that is engaging and informative on this platform. Use photos and videos to liven it up and maintain an active presence, posting 1-2 x per week

Take advantage of the new timesaving feature that allows you to repost Instagram Stories to Facebook.

Depending on what kind of products or services your business has, you can utilize special features that Facebook offers. For example, there are options to include a menu or services list where you could showcase a spa offering.

Click on the Insights tab at the top of your Facebook Page to see how your audience is engaging. This will show you how many people you are reaching, and which content is resonating best. This step is imperative if you want to learn what works and continually improve your social content strategy.

Community Set Up

Respond to any Facebook messages or comments as promptly and professionally as possible - checking this three times a day (morning, lunchtime, before end of business) will ensure you keep on top of it.

Join relevant industry groups to see what people are talking about. There are Facebook Groups for just about anything, so search your specialty and location and find a good fit for your business.

Groups like Salon Owners Support Group or Empowering Salon and Spa Owners exist on Facebook to lift fellow owners up, share tips, find solutions to issues related to your business, and more.

Pinterest

Why Use Pinterest?

Pinterest is a visual platform that allows users to gather inspiration on subjects that they are interested in and collect them on specialized Boards. 60% of Pinterest users are women, which makes it an engaging place for spas, salons and medspas who cater predominantly to women as well. Despite being slightly smaller than the other social media platforms, Pinterest users are three times more likely to click through to a company’s website than any other social platform.

Profile Set Up

Pinterest then requires you to give some details about what industry you are in, and what your goals are for using their platform. It will follow up with a step-by-step form to ask further questions about your industry and advertising on Pinterest - it is fine to select that you do not know at this time.

  • Create a Pinterest business account. Use the Pinterest form to enter your business name, website, location, and language details.
  • Describe your business. Select your focus and goals for using Pinterest.
  • Build a bio. Use a similar bio to Instagram or Facebook. It should be short but engaging and informative.
  • Add a profile picture. The dimensions for a Pinterest profile picture are 160 x 160 pixels and it will be cropped circular. A logo is perfect for this.

Utilize Analytics to view impressions, engagements, clicks, views and so much more. You can also select specific date ranges and view all your pins or only the pins saved from your website.

TikTok

Why Use TikTok?

TikTok is the up-and-coming short video platform that is heating up faster than the best curling iron. TikTok beauty content that goes viral has been generating serious sales for brands. For example, Deciem’s The Ordinary recently saw a 426% sales spike in its AHA 30% + BHA 2% Peeling Solution after the product caught on among the platform’s acne-positive teens.

Profile Set Up

Setting up your profile is simple and easy on TikTok and you can use many of the elements you already have created for your Instagram and Facebook profiles. These steps are meant to be followed on mobile, as this is a mobile-friendly app.

  • Download the TikTok app.
  • Sign up for a TikTok account.
  • Create a username. Choose a username that directly identifies your brand and ideally matches your other social handles.
  • Add a profile picture. Set a profile picture that will make your brand easy to recognize, including the logo or brand name. The dimensions for this photo are 200 x 200.
  • Add a profile picture. Set a profile picture that will make your brand easy to recognize, including the logo or brand name. The dimensions for this photo are 200 x 200.

Key Takeaways

Using the tools you learned here, setting up your profiles should be a stress-free process that doesn’t leave you needing a deep-tissue massage. Taking the time to build these correctly, in the beginning, will mean that you can focus on creating engaging content and using the platforms to their full potential to drive meaningful business results.
