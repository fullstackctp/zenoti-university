Driving Revenues by Increasing Average Spend

Introduction

“I think I want to make less money,” said no one ever. We love what we do. It’s creative, innovative, and most importantly, it makes people look and feel good. And we’re all about feeling good and finding great, but this is also a business. And a lucrative business, with over $57 billion in salon and spa sales generated in 2019 (Professional Beauty Association report).

There is clearly money to be made in this industry, and the tools to do so are already at your disposal. Even if you hate the constant coaching on rebooking and selling retail, those strategies are not going away, and they are proving to be more necessary than ever before. That’s why you need a defined plan to increase your revenue by improving your average spend.

In contrast to above, “I know I want to grow my revenue,” is uttered by every salon or spa owner/manager. The best starting place for a goal like this is to know your numbers. Where are you currently at with the Key Performance Indicator of average spend. And where do you want to be after a set amount of time?

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are measurements you create to demonstrate how effectively your salon or spa is achieving key business goals.

Do you currently know the average yearly spend for your clients? How about the average spend for guests at the time of check-out? These metrics are essential when evaluating opportunities for driving revenue.

When strategizing on average spend, think about the two categories in this way:

  1. Average Spend Per Guest: “On average, how much did guests spend this year?”
  2. Average Spend Per Visit: “On average, how much did guests spend today?”

These two categories have obvious cross-over, as any efforts to increase your average spend per visit (also referred to as average ticket) will also benefit your average spend per guest. Implementing the strategies in this course will help your business grow revenues across the board.

What you’ll learn:

  • Three Strategies for Increasing Average Spend Per Guest
  • Three Strategies for Increasing Average Spend Per Visit

Did you know?

  • The primary income stream for salons, spas, or barber shops, is service revenue, which hovers around 90%.
  • Your business plan is not “once and done”. Revisit your plan on a regular basis to stay on track with your revenue goals.
  • Any goals you set need to be SMART, i.e., they should be Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant and Time-bound.

Three Strategies for Increasing Average Spend Per Guest

When we speak about “average spend per guest” we are referring to one guest and their annual spend with your business. The strategies below will help you increase the average amount spent by your guests on a yearly basis.

1. Increase Frequency of Visits

2. Cater to High-Spenders

3. Offer and Promote High-End Services

Before you dive into the list above, you should review some key considerations about your guest base. You don’t want to focus heavily on average spend if your guest base is stagnant, shrinking, or lacking in loyalty.

Guest Acquisition: One of the most expensive undertakings a business will incur is guest acquisition. In fact, it can cost 5X more to attract a new guest, than it does to retain an existing one. This means after working to acquire new guests, you’ll definitely want to work even harder to retain them.

Guest Retention: Increasing retention rates by just 5% can improve profits by 25% to 95% (Forbes). With the average retention percentage at just 30% (Summit Salon Business Center), there’s plenty of opportunity to increase retention. Do not let 7 out of every 10 guests slip through your doors without a return visit.

Guest Loyalty: Loyal guests spend more money. Zenoti customer data shows the top 20% of customers (by spend) account for 66% of a salon or spa’s total revenue, and the top 60% of customers account for a whopping 95% of all revenues! Your loyal guests are willing to spend more, so help them do just that.

1. Increase Frequency of Visits

Think about the guests you saw four months ago. Have you seen them walk back through your doors? Think back to the end of their appointment, you’ve swept up their hair, escorted them to the coat room, and chatted as you approached the desk. In those last few minutes with that guest, did you remember to invite them back in? If you forgot, it may have cost you.

As salon professionals you understand the importance of rebooking. What you may not know is that the industry average for Frequency of Visit (FOV) is 4.88 times per year (BehindtheChair.com). So guests are visiting quarterly, even though most service providers recommend they return every 6-8 weeks to keep their desired look. Ultimately, guests are stretching out their appointments to save money and/or time. Unfortunately, their hair—and your bank account—are paying the price.

See how quickly a couple more visits each year adds up:

Plus, if you can boost FOV to 8 visits per year, you will double your revenue without the expense associated to acquire new guests. More visits will also mean more opportunities to promote service enhancements and retail sales, which adds to your profit.

Getting guests to rebook before they leave your shop lets your service providers control the appointment cadence, and the guest is less likely to stretch them out over longer periods of time. Rebooking is one of the most powerful ways to increase FOV. Guests are less tempted to go elsewhere when they are all set with their next appointment before they head out for the rest of their day.

HERE ARE SOME PROVEN METHODS TO INCREASE FREQUENCY OF VISITS:

Leverage expiration dates to help increase FOV. A guest may come in early to avoid losing out on a discount you’ve offered.

Activate in-salon offers during low traffic seasons. Extra savings may entice a guest to get in before the offer ends.

2. Cater to High-Spenders

Every service provider has high-spenders they look forward to seeing on their schedules. These clients are not afraid to book an expensive service, and they’ tend to listen to your style and maintenance recommendations. And heck, they’re usually the best tippers, too. But did you know their value to the business and provider go beyond the easy upgrade and tip they leave behind?

Zenoti reports 80% of revenue comes from the top 20% of your highest-spending guests.

Simply put, these are your besties, and it’s important to keep them in mind when making decisions about your business. The best way to retain your best guests is to foster their loyalty. You want your business to be synonymous with their beauty and wellness needs. Implementing tiered loyalty programs, where the more they spend, the richer the rewards, will show these guests how much you value them. Plus, data shows loyal guests spend more each year. For instance, 41% of consumers are more likely to do their holiday shopping with brands they love (Yotpo).

In 2015, Delta Airlines made headlines when they retooled their frequent-flier program to favor passengers who buy the priciest tickets instead of those who fly the most miles. It was intended to lure higher-spending business travelers, who often book flights on short notice and pay more than bargain-hunting leisure travelers. And it worked. Delta Airlines saw an increase of 13.4% in the following four years and is now ranked #2 for airlines in the U.S. (Yahoo News).

Your high-spend guests act as brand ambassadors, they feel proud to be a part of your salon/spa community, they speak highly of you to friends and family, generating many referrals.

OTHER WAYS YOU CAN ENGAGE YOUR HIGH SPENDERS:

When they reach a specific threshold of spend, treat them to luxury GWP offers.

Encourage package bookings. Your high-spend guests will not mind paying the cost up front, and its guaranteed revenue for the future.

Market to your top 20% of guests when you launch new products and services to showcase your innovation and dedication to evolving trends.

Encourage their participation in gift card promotions. They likely have a network like-minded pals, and those are future guests you would love to take care of.

Your high-end guests account for the majority of your revenue. And what do your high-end guests really want? High-end services, of course! Learn more in the next section.

3. Offer and Promote High-End Services

What’s the least expensive service you offer? Perhaps a lip wax, nail polish change, or a color toner? Can you make a living performing only these services? On your busiest days, would you be excited to do 8 hours' worth of lip waxes? If you answered “no”, it’s because you know you can’t survive on low-price services alone. High-end services are the key to good wages.

Offering and promoting high-end services is so impactful it can increase both average spend per guest and average spend per visit. Specialty services not only offer amazing results, they also pack a revenue punch. Booking a high-end service means you’re selling blocks of time for a higher profit. So it follows, a day booked with high-end services is a day with high-earnings.

Which day do you prefer?

  • 8 hours booked with $60 haircuts (30-minute duration) = $960
  • 8 hours booked with $300 smoothing treatments (2-hour duration) = $1,200

Often guests book services with little direction and may book a standard service as a placeholder. You should never assume a guest knows what is best for them and your service providers should feel empowered to suggest high-end services that are going to provide amazing results. For instance, the average facial costs $65-$90, and a HydraFacial averages $199-$300 per service. By recommending a HydraFacial, you just sold your time for 3X more revenue than the classic service.

You may think your guests are not interested in a luxury upgrade, yet a HydraFacial service is performed every 15 seconds around the world (HydraFacial).

EVEN MORE BENEFITS OF HIGH-END SERVICES:

They require maintenance and add to frequency of visits. You are securing future business as the guest needs to return for more treatments to see ongoing results. This is a clear path to increasing average annual guest spend.

For proper upkeep, specialty services require the use of salon professional products. Promoting high-end services is an easy gateway to increasing the sale of retail products.

High-end services attract an elite guest base. As you’ve learned, your top 20% makes up 80% of your business, and high-end services appeal to high-spenders!

Three Strategies for Increasing Average Spend Per Visit

When you consider your average spend per visit, think about it as capitalizing on the “ticket price” for each guest scheduled to walk through your doors. For example, each time block on your appointment book is an item to sell, and if a service provider is booked for the day, daily revenues can only grow by increasing the ticket price of each guest. The strategies below will help you increase each day’s average spend per visit.

1. Increase Product and Gift Card Sales

2. Strategically Implement Bundle Offers

3. Enhance your Online Presence and Shopability

Before you dive into the list above, take some time to know your numbers. When looking over invoices from the prior day, does anything stand out? Hopefully, you see cross-selling, upselling, and other ways the spend was increased for your guest visits. Maybe you recommended a deep conditioning treatment or took the time to shop the retail wall with the guest before saying goodbye. If there is little of that to be found, you are in the right place to develop the right strategies.

It’s important to develop friendly relationships with your guests, but do not forget this is your business. To increase average spend per visit, you need to be comfortable with pivoting a conversation from, “What’s new with you?” to “What’s new to buy in the salon? I’d love to take a look before I leave today.”

Keep these things in mind as you go through this information:

  • Are your employees trained to recommend options that will raise a ticket price at the time of check-out?
  • Is your space “shopable” and are guests enticed to purchase items for themselves or as gifts?
  • Do you have the right selling strategies in place so you and your team can boost revenues today?

1. Increase Product and Gift Card Sales

Do you know anybody that does not use shampoo? Any women that do not wear makeup, at least on occasion? Probably not many. We all use products. If your guest is not purchasing these products from you, they are purchasing them from somewhere.

The average U.S. hair salon services revenue makes up 92% of provider income. The other 8% is retail sales. However, the most successful salons boast a retail percentage of 14 - 18% (ModernSalon.com). This is not a happy accident. Leaders in the industry have figured out that retail sales are a profitable addition to their core service offerings.

It goes beyond the dollars. Retail sales have a direct correlation with retention. Based on a study conducted by Sam Villa, for each product sold there’s a 30% chance the guest will return. If your guest purchases three products from you, they are most likely yours for life. The science behind this comes down to trust and brand loyalty.

Take a Closer Look: The Art of Hair in Tulsa, Oklahoma, saw a 24% increase in product sales year over year. Their contributing factor is to offer four retail suggestions, compared to just one or two like most salons. These suggestions are reiterated at check-out so the guests can re-create their results at home (Salon Today).

EVEN MORE BENEFITS OF GIFT CARDS:

Gift cards are another easy way to increase spend per visit and your daily sales. Gift card sales provide proven revenue. Plus, gift card recipients have the potential to generate higher revenue in a single visit, as Zenoti reports that 65% of guests spent 40% more than the value of the card they received.

Gift cards can also drive sales during unforeseen circumstances. During the COVID closures, a strategic Zenoti customer put their focus on driving gift card sales online. They were able to recover $35,000 in direct revenue, despite the salon location not being open for business. For all the benefits of selling gift cards please refer to the gift card lesson.

2. Strategically Implement Bundle Offers

Everyone loves a great deal. When you shop and you find a great deal you feel good. You are less guilty about spending the money, and you are more likely to share your shopping experience with friends and family so that they too can benefit.

The salon and spa industry can capitalize on bundles in both a service capacity, as well as a retail capacity. This strategy boosts total revenue by increasing the amount spent on each transaction. Done well, product bundling is a win-win that increases your revenue and keeps your inventory moving.

Bundled services reward returning guests and encourage new guests to stick with their appointments. This is particularly helpful in the case of corrective services, where it can take a few appointments for guests to start seeing the results. Or for services that require regular maintenance, like manicures.

Consumers love a great deal! In fact, most guests expect discounts and offers. By comparing bundled to ‘a la carte’ prices, guests will feel they have found a bargain, even if they’re spending more money upfront.

HERE ARE SOME POPULAR WAYS TO BUNDLE

Bundle products to get a guest to increase their purchase that day. Tricoci Salon and Day Spa saw a 40% increase in their private label sales when they offered Buy 2, Get 1 Free for a two-week time frame.

Bundles can also make add-on’s more tempting. “Book a deep conditioning treatment in the salon today and receive a complimentary conditioner to take home”. This can be a great introduction to a new service and/or new product.

Bundle products to help move inventory. For example, when pairing a best-selling product with a weaker selling product you gain exposure of the less popular item. If it is an item you are hoping to deplete and discontinue, this is a great way to get rid of unwanted inventory.

Offer service packages, where a guest buys multiple services upfront. This will ensure that your guest is loyal to your business, and helps you anticipate their frequency of visits for the year. It also boosts the guest experience by expediting the checkout and rebooking process.

3. Enhance your Online Presence and Shopability

We have all accessed a bad website or mobile app, felt frustrated, and left. Perhaps you browsed a restaurant menu, only to find there was no way to reserve a table online. Or you previewed a spa menu only to learn there was no online booking option. If this has happened to you, you know about poor online presence.

Your business has control over the homepage of your website or mobile app—and it better make a good first impression—just as if a guest was walking through your front door!

  • You can incorporate buttons for “quick rebook” or “book now” to emulate the front desk greeting a walk-in guest.
  • You can set-up the booking flow in the way it was always intended, from offering high-end services and asking about add-ons.
  • You can ensure appointment confirmations and reminders are sent via the guest’s preferred channel—email or text.

Do not underestimate the power of your online presence, as 61% of shoppers would stop purchasing from a retailer if it had flawed website functionality (Klarna).

Every time one of your guests books an appointment using a self-service method (mobile application or webstore) they are shopping your business. The more accessible their options are for upgrades and add-ons, the more likely they will book a more expensive service, without the service provider even needing to pitch the benefits. Zenoti reported a 20% increase in average ticket when guests booked themselves versus calling to book.

THE IMPACT OF A STRONG ONLINE PRESENCE

When guests can book online using their computer or mobile device, you are essentially opening your doors 24/7.

According to a case study performed by a Zenoti customer, up to 91% of guests book appointments 12 days in advance when given an option of self-service booking from anywhere, anytime.

Having their books filled up in advance also allows you to adjust appointments to maximize your time blocks.

Customers who book online visit at least one more time per year and book 10 days sooner than customers who call in.

Added visits from online bookings will increase frequency of visits and your service providers will sell more blocks of time over the course of a year.
