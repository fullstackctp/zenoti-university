How Gift Cards Can Help Your Business

Gift cards can significantly increase revenue and bring new guests through your doors. They can turn existing guests into brand evangelists and expand your audience through their influence.

Gift cards are hugely popular, ranking in the US as the second-most given gift. They are the most-wanted gift by women and the third-most wanted by males. More than 70% of all US consumers claimed to have purchased a gift card as a present during recent holiday seasons. It is vital for your business to capture a share of this opportunity. If you don’t offer gift cards, you might lose out to another salon or spa that does.

But it’s about more than simply having gift cards available. It’s about creating an experience. That’s why we’re sharing examples of creative ways to expand your gift card horizons, motivate purchases, and make gift cards an important tool in the ongoing success of your business.

Attract New Guests with a BOGO Promotion

A salon offers a ‘Buy One Get One’ (BOGO) spring promotion for its gift card program. When a guest buys a gift card for $100, they get a bonus value of $20 added to it.

Jane, a loyal customer is motivated by the special offer and the beautiful packaging that she sees at the salon and decides to buy her friend, Carmen, a salon gift card for her birthday.

11% of customers are more likely to visit a store they otherwise wouldn’t have because they’ve received a gift card.

Jane goes to Carmen’s birthday party with a beautiful gift bag from the salon and presents it to her friend. People notice the brand and develop an interest in it. Carmen has never been to this salon before and now she’s excited to go!

Carmen makes an appointment at the salon the following week. She enjoys the visit and books a subsequent appointment.

Over the course of the two visits, she samples a service she wouldn’t have otherwise tried (for example, a balayage in addition to a haircut) and spends $200 ($120 gift card value and an additional $80 to get two great services, instead of just a haircut). She is happy with the experience and becomes a loyal customer. In general, 65% of customers spend close to 40% more than the value of the card they received.

What’s the impact?

Overall, the gift card program and a small promotional investment of $20 has helped the salon expand its brand awareness, gain a happy new customer, and earn back much more in revenue than the original investment.

Promote to Local Businesses for New Marketing Opportunities

A new office complex has opened nearby containing many small businesses, such as a physician’s office, dental office, law firm, and accounting firm. This is a great opportunity to connect with neighboring businesses and market to their employees with a special gift card offering.

The salon manager, Katie, promotes gift cards with the business owners and offers a bulk purchase discount. Two of the businesses decide to purchase gift cards as holiday gifts for their employees. Between the two companies, 50 employees are now aware of the salon and can try its services.

What’s the impact?

Of the 50 employees that redeemed gift cards, 20% of them really liked the experience, proximity, and convenience. So, it’s likely that 10 of those people will convert into regular guests.

Overall, the discount offered for the initial bulk purchase paid off in the long-term by helping the salon to capture new market opportunities and grow awareness. This results in significant return on investment, even if only a portion of the gift card recipients become regular guests.

Win Back an Unhappy Guest with a Complimentary Gift Card

You can use gift cards to create positive outcomes in difficult situations.

For example, Steve visits your spa for a massage treatment and is left feeling unsatisfied with the experience. He visits the front desk and asks for his money back. Instead of a refund, the front desk team offers Steve a gift card for a free massage on a future visit.

What’s the impact?

Overall, giving Steve a gift card in this scenario keeps the money in the spa and creates another chance for the spa to wow him and win him back. It also makes him more likely to spread the word to others that his potentially negative visit was resolved positively.

Provide Convenience with Digital Gift Cards

Selling gift cards online is a must-have convenience for your consumers. This not only lets clients easily give gifts to others, it also lets their friends and family give them a gift they know your client will love – all from the convenience of their computer or mobile device.

Consider Felicity, a loyal guest, who wanted to purchase a gift for a loved one. Felicity knew your salon was closed due to a COVID lockdown, but she chose to buy a digital gift card anyway. That way, her friend had something to look forward once your salon reopened.

What’s the impact?

In this scenario, digital gift cards helped your business make up lost revenues, while also helping Felicity to conveniently treat her friend and still support your salon during its closure. Zenoti customers earned an 88.6% increase in gift card sales during the COVID lockdown.

How to Create a Successful Gift Card Program

Let’s review the most important elements and aspects to consider when building out your gift card program.

Decide on Your Format - Physical or Digital

Gift cards are available in both physical and digital formats, and most savvy businesses leverage both options to maximize appeal to different types of customers and to suit different gift giving occasions. Major chains such as Starbucks, Chipotle, and Chili’s, plus most major beauty and wellness establishments, offer both digital and physical gift cards.

Click an image to learn more.

Physical Gift Cards

The right visuals are everything. Attractive gift cards placed at the checkout counter can encourage purchases. Some salons go a step further and offer beautiful envelopes and bags to carry the gift card, along with information or promotional offers. These gift packages, which often carry the salon or spa name and logo can serve as great marketing tools, especially when these gifts are given at an event.

Digital Gift Cards

On the other hand, digital gift cards offer convenience and have become a very significant portion of sales of gift cards overall. One of Zenoti’s top customers implemented a digital gift card program and found that within 3 months, 52% of gift cards were being purchased online.

Millennials are particularly attracted to digital gift cards, with 55% interested in giving one, and 67% interested in receiving one.

Digital gift cards are not only easy to buy on your computer or phone, they also make it convenient for the recipient to redeem using an app or card reader to speed up the checkout process.

Gift cards purchased online can also be personalized and customized in more ways than a physical one, including gifting a service or product rather than a dollar amount and sharing a note or sentiment. This customization is one of the reasons digital gift cards are more versatile.

Customize Your Cards

Set yourself up for success and allow customization in your gift cards. Some guests want to share a favorite service with friends and family, while others want to gift a dollar amount. Providing flexibility in the type of gift offered will drive more sales. Allowing the gift card buyer to customize the gift card adds a further purchase incentive as they can still add personalization for the recipient.


If a friend loves massages, gifting them one at your favorite spa (and maybe even going with them) makes it more personal than receiving a monetary gift card. Adding a personalized design or message on a digital gift card also makes the experience more satisfying for the gift giver who may miss being able to deliver it in person.

Make Them Easy to Purchase

Put Gift Cards Front-and-Center Online

Gift cards should be easy to purchase from your mobile app or webstore. If customers must hunt for it on your website, they may give up before they find it. On the other hand, if someone is visiting your website and they see gift cards placed front and center, they may be inspired to buy one even if that was not the original reason for their visit. Zenoti customer, EvelineCharles Salon and Spa proudly displays gift cards as a “Shop Online” drop-down directly on the homepage of their website.

Encourage Gift Card Purchases at the Front Desk

Create a beautiful card rack at the front desk and let the visual appeal do the selling for you. Remember, these are gifts so offer sleek envelopes or attractive boxes to house the gift card and remove the packaging step from your customer’s busy to-do list. And remember to keep the display and packaging on brand. Every element of your business should reflect the brand logo and color scheme wherever possible.

Go Beyond the Holiday Season

You can also get creative and promote gift giving for a variety of other events. For example, here are some seasonal promos you might consider:

  • Spring/Prom Promo:  Get a fresh haircut, manicure, or waxing service to be ready for the new season or big event.
  • Summer Fun/Memorial Day Promo: Get a new hair style or enjoy a spa day to celebrate all things summer.
  • Fall/Back to School Promo:  Indulge in some self-care to kick off the school year.

Motivate Your Staff

Get your staff on board with your gift card program by involving them!

Here are some great ways to increase motivation:

  • Establish Metrics: Share clear sales goals for the program so employees know what to work toward.
  • Set Up for Success with Training: Help them gain confidence about the new program and ensure they understand exactly how the program works, including what the gift cards can and can’t be used for.
  • Show Employees What’s in it for Them: How does your staff benefit when customers purchase and redeem gift cards? This includes personal benefits (such as a friendly sales competition) or team benefits in higher revenue. Everyone wins when there are more customers and a stronger business!
