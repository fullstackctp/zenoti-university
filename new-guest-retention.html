Trust is earned when actions meet words. Your current guests are returning because you earned their trust through the service you provide and the quality of the recommendations you give. And they demonstrate this appreciation by rebooking, referring, trying new services and products, and generally… spending more money.

Always Know your Numbers

Knowing your guest retention rates is the best way to measure the strength of your business.

Tracking the number/percentage of new and repeat guests that had a service and are booked to return within a certain timeframe (typically 90 days) is a tried-and-true way to measure the quality and consistency of the technical skills and customer experiences you deliver. Knowing these numbers is so vital – no one on your team gets to claim – “I’m just not a numbers person!” Share this information regularly and coach your employees to work toward the benchmarks you set.

Always Deliver the Best Experience

Just as the popular saying goes, your guests may not remember the exact service you provided, but they will always remember the way you made them feel. Which means, your business must always deliver an end-to-end experience that leaves your guests delighted throughout their time at your salon or spa. The time it takes to create a client experience cycle and train your staff to consistently deliver it is well worth the effort. Never leave this to chance!

Always Provide High-Quality Service

Your guests simply assume they will get the best, most cutting-edge service – it’s your area of expertise, after all. But again, never leave this to chance. Like professionals in any field, your providers will develop and improve their technical skills with ongoing training and education. That means, owners and managers need to create an educational calendar that will keep their technicians at the top of their game and excited to be working in a learning environment.

Never Take your Guests for Granted

Even the most loyal guest can drift off to another salon or spa if they feel they are no longer as appreciated as they should be. Never give your guests the motivation to look around for a new place by taking them for granted. In fact, you should have specific rewards and promos created just for these loyal clients. Plus, pay extra attention to the comments they share during a service or the feedback ratings they provide. A long-running client may help you see situations that are no longer operating at their peak, as it’s easy to overlook little things when you are in it all the time. Continue to foster the great relationships you’ve built with your retained guests!

Loyal Guests Provide Important Feedback

We’ve all had our bad days. Maybe a color formulation didn’t take right, or sensitive skin caused an adverse reaction during a treatment. It happens. The good news is when those bad days hit, loyal guests are more likely to give you another chance. They also offer valuable feedback over their lifetime of visits that will help you improve your business, address a problem before it grows, or coach a team member on their performance. A long-running client may help you see areas that are no longer operating at their peak, as it’s easy to overlook things you are exposed to all the time.

1. Offer Loyalty Programs

New guests often come in with coupons or expecting a discount. They pay less than your loyal guests at checkout, and then 7 out of 10 times, you never see them again.

Your regular guests follow healthy habits like purchasing retail and rebooking their appointments — but they frequently pay full price and end up feeling under appreciated.

It's time to start treating your regular guests with the recognition and rewards they deserve. And a loyalty program can help you do just that.

An effective loyalty program keeps guests coming back. They can be as simple or as robust as you want. A business can reward guests based on their spend amounts or certain behaviors, such as providing feedback, rebooking an appointment, or referring a friend.

4. Personalize Marketing Campaigns

Businesses need marketing. Even the world’s most successful companies invest extraordinary amounts in their marketing efforts to continually increase their customer base and entice existing customers. Your guests may need a push or a reminder to return for their next service, especially when there are so many other competing thoughts and priorities in their lives.

Traditional marketing methods include newspaper advertising, flyers, or mailers. While these can all be effective in attracting new guests, they can also be costly, difficult to track, and hard to target. Personalized, retention-focused marketing programs help you to deepen guest engagement and build loyalty. You'll find there is a significant amount of revenue to tap into, but only if you put the right campaigns in place.

Personalized marketing makes a difference, as it did for Do the Bang Thing Salon in Denver, Colorado. Their impressive results include: 280 recovered guests, 16% revenue uplift, and 15% of recovered guests booked follow up visits.

The key to driving the right campaign to the right people at the right time is to create target segments based on a wide-range of categories.

Another benefit of personalized marketing is it tends to be more cost effective because the grouping guests based on buying patterns and preferences determines which offers are relevant. Target segments allows marketing to groups based on a wide-range of categories, such as demographics, service history, memberships, spends, and visits. It also helps you to avoid costly mistakes, like sending a coupon for a beard trim to women. Yikes!

5. Create a Culture of Rebooking

A closer look at any high-performing spa or salon will reveal they have a strong commitment to rebooking. And not just at the front desk, but also with the service providers. Obviously, rebooking is good for your business, but it also benefits your clients in a big way. Of course, they want to look their best or maintain their improved well-being, but to do that they need your rebooking recommendations. If it helps to get over your hesitancy, think of it as “educating the client” on why it’s best for them to make their next appointment before they leave the shop.

Rebooking means a guest booked their next appointment on the same day of the service they just received.

To qualify as a rebooked appointment, guests can:

  1. book before they leave your shop,
  2. book online from home, or
  3. book by calling you before days’ end.

Even with these options, do not leave rebooking to chance! Ensure every guest is offered the convenience of rebooking their next appointment before they go about the rest of their day.

Stick to the Script

Something as important as rebooking deserves more than a simple, “Would you like to rebook?” It’s just too easy for your clients to reply, “No thanks.” The better option is to use a script that focuses on the benefits of booking before they leave. Prepping a script and practicing with your team is worth it, as it’s estimated that clients who rebook visit about 50% more often than those who don’t.

Prepping a script like this is helpful for training your staff:

“To maintain your great results, we’d love to see you back in *insert time* weeks. Does the same day and time work well for you? I can reserve that date now, and if anything changes, we can easily adjust the appointment.”

TIPS FOR REBOOKING

A culture of rebooking means every guest gets encouraged to rebook after every appointment. Don’t wing it!

Think of rebooking as “educating” the client on how to maintain their results, not as “selling” them on their next appointment.

Provide a specific date and time when recommending their next appointment, rather than an open-ended, “What day works for you?”

Offer incentives to guests, like additional loyalty points, when they rebook their appointments.

Offer incentives to your service providers and front desk staff to build even more motivation for rebooking clients.

When a guest cannot rebook before leaving, always share how easy it is to rebook online or using your customer mobile app—often in a single step!

6. Improve Service Provider Retention

It goes without saying that spas and salons are nothing without their service providers. Businesses rely on their training and talent!

Staff turnover doesn’t only hurt morale in your salon, it also impacts your business. The cost of losing a team member can add up to thousands of dollars. Even more frightening, 76% of guests confessed to feeling more loyal to their service provider than to the business, so the odds of retaining a guest if their service provider decides to leave are less than 25%.

Face turnover issues head-on with a plan in place:

  • Develop a company culture that people want to be a part of.
  • Recognize their achievements, especially when they hit the goals and benchmarks you’ve set for them.
  • Reward them for their great work with unexpected treats and perks.
  • Avoid burn out by encouraging -- or even requiring – regular breaks, rotating duties, and days off.

Next time you detect a red flag for a high performing or influential team member, act fast! Better yet, be proactive with your employee retention efforts.
