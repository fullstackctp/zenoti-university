How Gift Cards Can Help Your Business

Gift cards can significantly increase revenue and bring new guests through your doors.

How?

By turning existing guests into brand evangelists and expanding your audience through their influence.

But it’s about more than simply having gift cards available. It’s about creating an experience. That’s why we’re sharing examples of creative strategies to expand your gift card horizons, motivate purchases, and make gift cards an important tool in the ongoing success of your business.

Gifting Strategy

A salon offers a Buy One Get One (BOGO) spring promotion for its gift card program. When a guest buys a gift card for $100, they get a bonus value of $20 added to it.

Jane, a loyal customer is motivated by the special offer and the beautiful packaging that she sees at the salon and decides to buy her friend, Carmen, a salon gift card for her birthday.

Jane goes to Carmen’s birthday party with a beautiful gift bag from the salon and presents it to her friend. People notice the brand and develop an interest in it. Carmen has never been to this salon before and now she’s excited to go! Jane has now not only created brand awareness with her friend but also with the attendees at the party.

11% of costumers aged 18 to 34 are more likely to visit a store they otherwise wouldn’t have because they’ve received a gift card.

Carmen makes an appointment at the salon the following week. As she has never been to the salon before, this is a new guest opportunity.

She enjoys the visit and books a subsequent appointment. Over the course of the two visits, she samples a service she wouldn’t have otherwise tried (for example, a balayage in addition to a haircut) and spends $200 ($120 gift card value and an additional $80 to get two great services, instead of just a haircut).

She’s so happy that she becomes a loyal customer. The salon not only earns much more in revenue in the first two visits than the value of the gift card, but also gains a new customer. Demonstrating a very worthwhile investment from the BOGO spring promotion.

65% of customers spend close to 40% more than the value of the card they received.

Bulk Buy Strategy

A new office complex has opened nearby containing many small businesses, such as a physician’s office, dental office, law firm, and accounting firm. The salon manager promotes gift cards with the business owners and offers a bulk purchase discount.

Two of the businesses decide to purchase gift cards as holiday gifts for their employees.

Now all these employees are aware of the salon and can try its services. If they have a total of 50 employees and just 20% of them like the experience, the proximity and convenience will likely convert those 10 people into regular guests.

Turn a Negative into a Positive Strategy

Use gift cards to create positive outcomes in difficult situations.

Addressing unhappy guests: Often business owners will refund the cost of the service in the event of a negative experience. Giving the guest a gift card instead keeps the money in your business while giving you another chance to wow them and win them back.

Public health challenges: When guests aren’t able to attend in person due to a public health issue or a lockdown, you can sell gift cards with creative promos to make up for lost revenues.

Zenoti customers earned an 88.6% increase in gift card sales during the COVID lockdown.

The gift industry is huge, lucrative and growing very quickly. Gift cards are a popular part of this, ranking in the US as the second-most given gift by consumers, the most-wanted gift by women, and the third-most wanted by males. More than 70% of all US consumers claimed to have purchased a gift card as a present during the holiday season. It is critical to your business to capture a share of this opportunity. If you don’t offer gift cards, you might lose out to another salon or spa that does.
