How Gift Cards Can Help Your Business

Gift cards can significantly increase revenue and bring new guests through your doors by turning existing guests into brand evangelists and expanding the market through their influence.

But it’s about more than simply having gift cards available. It’s about creating an experience. That’s why we’re sharing examples of creative strategies to expand your gift card horizons and highlight how gift cards can be an important tool in the success of your business.

Gifting Strategy

A salon offers a Buy One Get One (BOGO) spring promotion for its gift card program. When a guest buys a gift card for $100, they get a bonus value of $20 added to it.

Jane, a loyal customer is motivated by the special offer and the beautiful packaging that she sees at the salon and decides to buy her friend, Carmen, a salon gift card for her birthday

Jane goes to Carmen’s birthday party with a beautiful gift bag from the salon and presents it to her friend. People notice the brand and develop an interest in it. Carmen has never been to this salon before and now she’s excited to go! Jane has now not only created brand awareness with her friend but also with the attendees at the party.

11% of costumers aged 18 to 34 are more likely to visit a store they otherwise wouldn’t have gone to because they’ve received gift card.

Carmen makes an appointment at the salon the following week. As she has never been to the salon before, this is a new guest opportunity for the salon.

She enjoys the visit and books a subsequent appointment, and over the course of the 2 visits, she experiences a service she wouldn’t have otherwise tried (for example, a balayage in addition to a haircut) and spends $200 ($120 gift card value and an additional $80 to get 2 great services, instead of just a haircut).

She’s so happy that she stays on a loyal customer. Thus, the salon earns much more in revenue in the first 2 visits than the value of the gift card, which makes it a worthwhile investment in addition to gaining a new customer.

65% of customers spend close to 40% more than the value of the card they received.

Bulk Buy Strategy

A salon finds out that a new office complex has come up nearby and many small businesses such as physician’s offices, dental offices, law firms, and accounting firms. The salon manager promotes gift cards with the business owners and offers a bulk purchase discount.

Two of the business decide to purchase gift cards as holiday gifts for all their employees

Now all these employees are aware of the salon and can experience its services. If they have a total of 50 employees and even if just 20% of them like the experience, the proximity and convenience will likely convert those 10 people regular guests.

Turn a Negative into a Positive Strategy

You can also gift cards to create positive outcomes in difficult situations.

Addressing unhappy guests: Often business owners will refund the cost of the service in the event of a negative experience. Giving the guest a gift card instead will keep the money in your business while giving you another chance to wow and win them back.

Public health challenges : When guests aren’t to attend in person due to a public health issue or a lockdown, sell gift cards with creative promos to make up for lost revenues.

Zenoti customers earned an 88.6% increase in gift card sales during the COVID lockdown.

The gifting industry is huge and lucrative and growing very quickly. Gift cards are highly popular, ranking in the US as the second-most given gift by consumers, the most-wanted gift by women, and the third-most wanted by males. More than 70% of all US consumers claimed to have purchased a gift card as a present during the holiday season. It is critical to your business to capture a share of this opportunity. If you don’t offer gift cards, you might lose the opportunity to another salon or spa that does.
