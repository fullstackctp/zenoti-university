What is Social Media Marketing?

Social media marketing is sharing content to promote your business and products on various social media platforms. It’s about meeting your guests where they’re at and engaging with them there.   Here’s a quick summary of the top social media platforms we covered in the Social Media Presence: Why It's Essential for Your Business course. 

Wellness-related content is popular across visual platforms including Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest. Therefore, we recommend focusing on these channels.  

There are five core pillars of social media marketing that we’ll be covering later in the course but first it’s important to understand why having a social media strategy is essential for your salon or spa.  

Let’s get started!

Why is it Important in the Beauty and Wellness Industry? 

Social media marketing is key in the beauty and wellness industry simply because of the sheer number of people who actively use it every day. There’s no other way to reach and engage with that many people in real-time.  

More than 80% of the 1 billion active users on Instagram use it to research products and services.  For salons, spas, and medspas, this presents incredible opportunities to elevate your online presence, increase engagement, and drive sales.

Social Media Marketing Pillars 

As we alluded to earlier in the course, social media marketing has five core pillars.  

  • Strategy
  • Create and Publish Content
  • Listen and Engage
  • Advertise
  • Analyze

1. Strategy  

The first step in any marketing plan is to know who you are, who you’re talking to, where you’ll reach them, and how.  

Define Your Brand Personality

Establish your page aesthetics and tone of voice to create a consistent look and feel that people will come to recognize as your brand.  

  • What are your brand colors?
  • What are your brand fonts?
  • What is your tone (funny, professional, trendy, etc.)?

You may have established this when you launched your business. If so, consider if your current aesthetic is still relevant? Is it reaching your ideal guests? Does it need a re-vamp?   If you haven’t established this, now is the time.

Take Boss Gal Beauty, for example. What does their page say to you?

They use a consistent, overall color palette so the feed looks cohesive. Think about it, if someone is looking to book a service with you that they hope will make them look and feel good - do your social media platforms convey that message to them?

Learn the 3 tips to boost your social media presence top here.

Know Your Audience

  • Who are you talking to?
  • What do they need and want to know?
  • What do you want them to know about you?
  • Which platform(s) are they using?

Set Up Your Team 

Define the roles and responsibilities for your team and ensure they have all the information they need. Consider: 

  • Who handles overseeing the strategy?
  • Who will be creating content and who will be publishing it? 
  • Who will be engaging with your audience? 
  • What is your posting and engagement cadence? 
  • Do the right people have access to the right information (passwords, etc.)? 

If these questions seem too big to answer right now, or you’re not sure where to start, head to the QuickStart Guide: Setting Up Key Social Media Platforms ZU course for insights, best practices, and easy to follow steps to set you up for success.  

Create a Content Calendar

This doesn’t have to be overly complex. It can be a simple Google sheet with the following: 

  • Platforms - Facebook, Instagram, etc.
  • Publish Dates - A weekly or monthly look at content to be published, based on your cadence answered above.
  • Content Type – Video, Reel, feed post, ad, etc.
  • Content Mix – How much of your content will be paid vs. organic, sales vs. education, etc.

Pro Tip: 

If you’re starting out, Hootsuite recommends the 80-20 rule: 

80% of your posts should inform, educate, or entertain your audience (this can include influencer marketing).

20% can directly promote your brand (this can include giveaways, promotions, and paid marketing).

Establish Goals

What are your social media goals and what are the metrics (also known as KPIs or key performance indicators) you will use to track your performance?  

If you’re feeling overwhelmed by that question, know it doesn't have to be some elaborate concept. It can be as simple as reaching a wider audience to grow brand awareness or generating more referral traffic to your website through social media. If you set metric to it, you make it a powerful goal.  

For example: 

If your goal is to increase your Instagram followers by 5% month over month, then you would document the number of followers you’re starting with and review that number, as well as the tactics you used, monthly to establish if your current plan is working to meet your goals or if you need to adjust.  

Sometimes a simple strategy is the best choice. Once you have an established plan and are seeing results, you can build it out further and include specific goals, metrics, and calendars for both paid and organic content (more on that later!). 

2. Create and Publish Content

Once you’ve established your foundation, it’s time to start creating. Use these tips as general guidelines and see module 5 for specific ideas.  

Provide Useful and Digestible Content  

Become a thought leader in the industry and increase bookings by providing content your audience wants.  

How do you know what they want? Ask and listen. 

Read your reviews, listen to what your front desk staff is talking to guests about, send out a survey, and keep your finger on the pulse of industry trends

3. Listen and Engage 

Building a brand on social media requires you to be active and post relevant content. Community is a two-way street that includes both proactive and reactive engagement.  

Proactive Engagement

Use features like stories, videos, lives, reels, etc. to create simple and cost-effective video content so your audience can engage with a real person. This is ideal for new launches, promotions, how-to’s, and free value-add services like education.  

Reactive Engagement

Use comments, shares, and industry hashtags to authentically connect with your ideal guest and stay connected with your audience.

Advertise 

For maximum reach, consider both organic and paid content. While they do intersect, the best way to think of them is that paid is a way to build your audience and organic is a tool to nurture existing followers.  

Organic Content 

Is all the free content you post (feed posts, stories, reels, etc.). It’s seen by a percentage of your followers, and anyone searching the hashtags or locations you include. Organic content is one of the best ways to convey your brand personality, educate about your products and services, stay engaged with your followers, and even drive sales.  

Visual social media platforms like Instagram are being used as virtual stores and using the feed as a product display.  

Take the widely popular cosmetics brand, Glossier for example. They make it convenient to shop for their products, directly from their feed. Users simply click on a picture to shop. 

Paid Content 

Paid ads and boosting posts are both forms of paid advertising. These are posts that you pay to boost or ads that you create, both are target specific audiences.  

Paid advertising includes boosting a post where you pay to have your post seen by a specific audience, awareness ads where you target a new audience to generate leads, competitor advertising where you target people who actively engage with other brands in your industry and remarketing or retargeting ads where you serve up an ad to someone who has previously been to your website or has engaged with your previous Facebook ads or Facebook page. 

One simple way to get started with paid content is creating a Facebook ad.  

These ads are highly customizable and budget friendly. You’re able to leverage Facebook’s data to set your audience and target your ideal demographic. You can also choose the ad’s placement from in-feed, to stories, to appearing on the right-hand side of the page, and more.  

Facebook ads are a great way to test out your audience and different types of content. You can try different demographics or ad placements and see which performs best.  

Ads can be as simple as an in-feed static image post. However, you may want to consider more dynamic options like Sephora’s video ad below or carousel images. Both have recently seen high engagement rates, so Facebook recommends this approach to catch your audience's attention.  

It is important to note that paid advertising doesn’t include paid influencer content. Yes, that will be a paid expense, but it is a separate strategy that we’ll explore in module 5.

5. Analyze

Social media strategy is nothing without goals, metrics, and a review process. This isn’t the boring part, and it doesn’t have to be tedious admin work.  

You don’t have to track everything. Track what’s important to you, based on your business goals and social media strategy.  

The metrics you watch may evolve over time as your business grows and your goals change. However, as a general guideline, here are our top recommendations for key metrics to track:  

If your goal is Awareness and Growth track... 

Audience Growth - Document the number of followers you’re starting with, then look at your new followers after a pre-determined amount of time (i.e., monthly, directly following a giveaway, or paid influencer post, etc.).  

To find out your audience growth rate percentage, divide the number of new followers by your total following and multiply by 100.  

100 new followers ÷ 1000 total followers x 100 = 10 new followers 

Reach and Impressions - Monitoring both reach and impressions will give you a good idea of how much awareness your social media efforts are bringing to your business. While similar metrics, reach is the number of people who saw your content, and impressions are the number of times your content was displayed.  

Pro Tip: Use built–in analytics on platforms like Instagram to see your top-performing organic content including posts, stories, reels, lives, etc. You can find this data by clicking “insights” on your profile. 

If your goal is Sales Conversion track... 

Click-Through Rate and Referral Traffic - Use built-in analytics on platforms like Instagram and Facebook to see how well your ads (also known as “promotions” on Instagram) converted to purchase or booking from your website. You can also use your website analytics to monitor referral traffic sources. Like click-through rate, this information tells you which content and source is driving traffic to your website.  

If your goal is Loyalty and Advocacy track... 

Engagement - Understand your engagement on both organic and boosted posts to learn how your content is performing so you can do more of what’s working, and less of what isn’t. Engagement metrics include likes, comments, and saves. Use all three metrics for a holistic understanding of engagement. 

For example, if a post has 10 likes, 100 comments, and 1000 saves, that is engaging content. If you only measured likes, you'll have missed how engaging that post was. 

Consider analytics as the key to unlocking your guest’s innermost needs and wants so you can maximize your efforts and serve up exactly what they’re looking for.  

Stay Open to Change

If your Facebook Ads aren’t driving website conversion or bookings, they aren’t working. If your guests aren’t engaged with the content you post on Instagram, it’s not working.

Social Media Marketing Tactics 

Now that we have all the foundations, it’s time to have some fun! Let’s look at a variety of social media tactics for your plan #Inspo.  

Giveaways 

Who doesn’t love a giveaway? Gift free products and services or connect with aligned brands for a co-marketing giveaway effort to reach an even wider audience. Consider this topical content that can be used to highlight new products or services, create brand awareness, and encourage engagement. Incorporate “tag a friend” and contesting to take your giveaways to the next level. Keep in mind, you want to include a clear CTA (call to action), so your audience knows what to do. 

Using The NOW massage boutique as an example, their Summer Giveaway, where they partnered with other beauty and wellness brands, generated over 200 comments compared to surrounding posts which generated 3- 50 comments. 

Gamify Your Feed 

Given the popularity of games, challenges, and memes, you can use trending topics to gamify your feed. Couple this with the fact that video and animation content perform well, use this strategy to engage your community and show off your personality. 

Can you challenge your audience to share their worst childhood hair photo and build out a contest around it? Or ask them to share a before and after video for a chance to be featured? How can you have fun with your audience with topical conversations?  

Let’s breakdown the elements of Zenoti customer, Depasquale The Spa’s Instagram post to see this concept in action.  

Gamification - The caption includes a clear CTA to tag @depasqualethespa for a chance to be featured. This gamifies the post and encourages your guests to share UGC (user generate content).   UCG - This is a re-post from their client’s TikTok account. Using UGC builds a strong community. More on that later.  

Provider Highlight - The post also includes a link to the provider’s account, highlighting their work and amplifying their profile.  

Brand Personality - Depasquale The Spa highlights their brand personality using language like “gorgeous” and emoji use. 🤍  

As you can see, you can achieve a lot in one post. And it doesn’t have to be hard!  

UGC

UGC (user-generated content) is an effortless way to gather organic content, solidify the bond with your guests (and providers!), and create referral traffic.  

Imagine you posed a photo, tagging your favorite brand, and they reshared it on their account. How would you feel? Words like seen, appreciated, welcomed, and #Famous come to mind.  

Use challenges, games, or a branded hashtag (as we discussed in the QuickStart Guide: Setting Up Key Social Media Platforms) as a CTA for your audience to share content that you can repurpose on your feed.  

Zenoti customer Head of the Pride encourages providers to post as a solution to unexpected cancellations.  

This strategy highlights providers, builds out UGC, fills their schedule, and uses topical content relevant to their guests.

“Every salon experiences a slow week or unexpected cancellations. We have a few ways that we try to fill up open spots on our stylists' schedules. We encourage our stylists to use social media channels to let their friends and followers know they have a service schedule opening available. Then we support them using our salon’s Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.”  

AMA

Host an AMA (ask me anything). This is a great way to engage your audience and gather valuable insights for future content. Create an evergreen campaign by hosting a weekly, monthly, or quarterly AMA and invite providers to take part. This increases your reach, adds a level of dynamics to the feature, and allows you to build out a longer-lasting campaign around this framework.  

Bonus Tip: Create both video and written content of your AMA series and share it across multiple channels.  

Social Media Takeovers and BTS (behind the scenes)

You can use in-house providers or yourself (as the business owner) as the subject of your takeover. This showcases the people behind the brand, connecting you to your audience on a deeper level. Use video and images to show BTS footage of your salon or spa to give potential new guests a look into your business. Build out an evergreen campaign of weekly or monthly takeovers to get your audience excited about your brand and upcoming content.  

BTS content doesn’t have to be limited to tours of your salon. Lunatic Fringe, a Zenoti customer, uses BTS content in a variety of ways, from color mixing images to process photos and capturing that “after” photo feeling.  

In this sample of their feed, you can see how they weave BTS content throughout.  

Paid Influencers

Typically, when building an influencer partnership, you will start with paid posts. But keep the long game in mind and consider paid influencer partnerships that include a social media takeover as a powerful strategy to build your audience.  

Consider Vital Proteins; they built their brand on influencers and now have a thriving influencer community. 

Pro Tip: When looking for influencers, consider authenticity with your brand and engagement on their platforms. Just because someone has 100K followers doesn't mean they’re the right fit.  

Brand Collaborations 

Different than a co-marketed giveaway or influencer partnership, brand collaborations are when you team up with one more business for an exclusive offer, promoted mutually across your platforms.   Key for reaching new potential guests, growing your social following, and creating buzz.   Get Creative -Build a summer-exclusive “ultimate spa package” in collaboration with a local restaurant, or winery.  Create an exclusive hair accessories line with a popular brand, like how skincare brand COOLA, partnered with Sugar Fina for their “Stay Tropical” gift set.
