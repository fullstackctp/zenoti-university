Attract and Retain Customers with An Effective Online Presence

It’s Thursday evening and you’ve just been reminded that it’s your turn to make the group plan for your meetup with friends at the weekend.

You want to plan something fun at a place that will really wow them, so you mentally prepare a list of different ideas, from restaurants, theatres, music shows, to a spa day. What do you do next?

You reach for your phone, or your laptop, and begin browsing.

Gone are the days of flipping through a newspaper or picking up the phone to call. Customers want access and they want it now. Website browsing is the most popular way to conduct business because of its efficiency and accessibility.

Less than 15 seconds. That’s the average time spent on a website. And that’s how long you have to capture someone’s attention. - The Daily Egg

People form an impression within 7 seconds of meeting someone new. Online it’s a little more generous at 15 seconds. That’s right, a quarter of a minute to win someone over on your landing page.

Imagine you’re accessing your company’s website for the first time. Ask yourself:

  • Is it clear what you do?
  • Is it too busy, or perhaps too bare?
  • Can you easily locate the key information a guest will need to inform their decision-making process?
  • Most importantly, is there a way for them to act (e.g., does “Book Here!” jump out in an obvious place)?

If you’re unsure about any of the answers to these questions, then this course is for you! Read on to learn about the key elements of creating an effective online presence.

Why is an Online Presence Important in The Beauty and Wellness Industry?

Websites may have once just been a digital business card but not anymore. Today, websites represent your entire brand identity. It is your business portfolio, advertising, and receptionist all rolled into one.

Therefore, the time and energy you invest in building a strong online presence can really help to propel your business to the next level. And on the other hand, it can really hinder your growth and customer acquisition if it is not representative of your brand or delivers a negative user experience.

The beauty and wellness industry is not exempt to the importance of having an online presence. In fact, because we are appointment based and sell a look, our web presence may be most important of all.

Across Zenoti customers, 29% of online booked appointments are from new guests.

Your website serves as your greatest tool for tapping into an entirely new audience base of prospective guests. Consider your experience from working at the front desk. Phone interactions with new guests typically take longer than conversations with an existing guest. This is because new visitors have questions (understandably so!) and for that reason like to access a website. A website enables them to have a browse and get a feel for the brand before they make the decision about whether it’s a good fit for them and their needs.

Once your website has caught their attention, they can take their time exploring without feeling like they are imposing on anyone or pressured to make a commitment. Unlike calling a receptionist, who may only have three minutes to book an appointment, a guest accessing your website and online booking platform has no time limit to shopping around. And that is a good thing!

Besides, do your receptionists really have time to talk on the phone with every new guest?

Your website is essentially a taste of your business, and the information on your page are your samples. They are being consumed by a lot of people, and you want to pass the test.
