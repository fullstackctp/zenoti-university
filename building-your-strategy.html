Building Your Strategy

The foundation of your strategy must start with your target audience or ideal customer. You are committed to embracing a deeper sense of power in your business and are excited to learn how to find more ease and joy in the process. Start with understanding who you are talking to by getting to know your ideal customer.

Before you skim past this section or generalize who this person is, we invite you to get out your pen and paper and set aside some time to consider the following:

Who is your ideal customer?

  • What is their age?

  • What is their gender?

  • Where do they live?

  • Where do they shop?

  • What websites do they visit?

  • Where do they get their information (example: online, word of mouth, etc)?

  • What do they do for a career?

  • What is their socio-economic situation?

  • What goal are they hoping to achieve (example: 70's bangs are back and they want them STAT)?

  • What is a “pain point” for them (example: they work shifts and have obscure availability so it’s hard for them to get an appointment during traditional hours)?

Now that you know your ideal customer it is time to consider your brand.

What is your brand?

  • What does your salon or spa specialize in (hot tip: it can be one thing! You don’t have to be for everyone)?

  • What are your hours?

  • Where is your location(s)?

  • What is your brand model (premium, affordable, family-friendly, niche, etc.)?

  • What is the overall feeling you want to convey?

  • What is your salon aesthetic and how does that carry forward to your marketing?

  • What is the experience you want clients to have with your brand?

Next, it is time to look at your delivery method.

Are you going to use digital or print or a combination of both? There’s no right or wrong answer or a “one-size-fits-all” method. Let’s look at options so you can make an educated decision on what is best for your business.

Digital

In 2021, to be a successful business you must have an online presence. This can be a simple website, Facebook page, and/or Instagram account, or it can stretch across multiple channels and include an in-depth professionally created website.

Digital marketing has many benefits, including reaching a wider audience, targeting specific users, and capabilities for future learning. With platforms like Zenoti, where all your data is centrally located, you can measure the effectiveness of your marketing campaign in a few easy clicks.

One of the most beneficial attributes of digital marketing is that it is interactive. You do not need Cover Girl sized budgets for your marketing spend or corporate executives to run a successful campaign. If your ideal customer gets all of their recommendations from Instagram, then that’s a great place to start. You don’t have to be everywhere or in every medium. Meet people where they’re at, and engage, engage, engage with them. Digital marketing is not passive, you connect with your clients through engagement. Aim to provide education, inspiration, and entertainment.

Take Glossier for example:

The beauty brand, valued at $1.2 billion, provides education and tips on Instagram that their customers can use. This positions the brand as a knowledge expert while boosting brand awareness and keeping Glossier top of mind for customers.

Print

Know your location. Is local marketing popular where your business is? Do people rely on the local paper? Local print marketing may be the place to meet your potential clients. Only you can answer that.

When it comes to print advertising, circulation and use are everything. So, before investing, confirm what the circulation numbers are and what revenues or return on investment (ROI) you can expect.

Time for another example. Let’s look at stylist Vidal Sassoon.

When you think of Vidal Sassoon , what comes to mind? The bob, exactly. Known for making the bob popular, the name evokes a feeling of heritage, timeless, and classic style. His notoriety came from working with celebrity clients and, back in the 1950s, he didn’t reach these clients through his Instagram page. However, if you’re looking for a modern-day Sassoon experience you will more than likely start with Sassoon.com.

From one cut to a product line, to salons and schools, the name alone tells customers to expect quality. If you’re looking for the best bob cut in town, you may head to Google or you may search out the nearest Sassoon Salon because the expectation is that is what you will get there. That is the power of a clear marketing strategy.
