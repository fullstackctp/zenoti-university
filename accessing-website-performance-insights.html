Accessing Website Performance Insights

Your website is up and running! Now what?

Once you’ve launched your website, you have access to data which can help you to better understand how it is performing. You’ll have insights into most visited pages and where people are bouncing off to help you identify what should be refreshed and optimized to drive the best results.

These insights provide a heat map for you to analyze the user behavior and the performance of your site at a glance.

“Hot” areas gained the most traffic, guests either clicked, stopped scrolling, or spent an extended time on a particular page or part of your website. “Cold” areas are those where a guest spent the least amount of time, they did not effectively capture guest attention. Knowing how your website performs helps you to find answers on how to get site visitors to convert to purchasing and/or booking.

With this knowledge you can continue to enhance and improve your website, and your business.

If you are choosing to feature Ecommerce on your website, you can also track the performance of product sales. Are your top sellers the same as those in-salon? Do the reviews reflect the number of sales? Of guests that purchased online, how many returned to purchase a second time?

Based on the performance on the website you can determine what products guests are drawn to, even when a service provider is not there to make a recommendation. Perhaps those products deserve a spotlight in-salon?

Overall, website performance insights serve as the “fly on the wall”. You suddenly have visibility into success rates and can better determine which promotions drive the most business, which part of the booking process loses the most guests and how many of your appointments are booked online. By understanding how guests interact with your website you can identify trends and optimize for further engagement.

Key Takeaways

Whether your guest is walking through the front doors of your salon or spa or visiting your website for the first time, they are forming a first impression of your brand. Failing to dedicate the time and energy into building a positive website experience leaves a lasting first impression - and not the good kind!

A guest decides in fifteen seconds if they are going to shop on your site, how are you going to make the most of those valuable seconds?
