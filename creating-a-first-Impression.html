Creating a First Impression

The experience a guest has with your website is even more important than the experience that they have in your center. This is because your website is open to the world 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. This means it has the capability to reach more people which is a great thing for your brand awareness, but have you ever stopped to consider what first impression those visitors get when they land on your home page?

Within the first fifteen seconds the user needs to know what you do and be convinced that you’re the best at it. Whether you are trying to attract business executives, busy parents, or savvy college students, you will not get far if your website experience is poor.

If your website is frustrating, the guest is not going to chance walking through your doors. If the experience is seamless and guest friendly, they will associate your salon with being guest centric as well.

Consider what else a user may see when they enter your landing page.

  • Does your website represent guest engagement?
  • Can they see what you’ve curated on your social media?
  • If guests are asking questions or providing feedback, are you responding so that your community can see your business is responsible and provides follow-through?

Digital interactions should mirror your in-person interactions. You would never think to not call an upset guest back, so why would you leave someone hanging online?

Finally, prospective guests want to see your photo gallery. If you do not have one, that can be a huge red flag. You sell a look, whether it is clear skin, beautiful nails, or healthy hair, if you are not showcasing your photos on your website, you are not promoting what it is that you do best.

Online presence is an amazing tool, but for every reward there is a risk. The risk? If your website is not designed properly, it will drive guests away from considering you in the future.
