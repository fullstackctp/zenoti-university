Why a Social Media Presence is Essential for Your Business

Looking to utilize the ever-growing medium of social media to support your business goals, but not sure where to start? This course is designed to help you understand what organic social media can do for you and why it is an important tool to help your business grow in a digitally driven world.

For a long time, businesses have relied on word of mouth and referrals to attract new customers and build a following. The influence people have on their friends in terms of choosing a salon or stylist has a big impact on how that company can bring in new customers.

Social media has exploded this trend and given people from all over the world the power to share their opinions on an ever-expanding scale. It is also an increasingly important source for building trust.

Consumers now look to visual platforms, such as Instagram and Pinterest, for health and wellness inspiration. So, knowing how to catch their attention and stand out is key to driving engagement with current and future customers. During this course we will go back to basics to build out a solid foundational understanding of social media practices. These core principles will help guide your social media strategy and allow you to create digital marketing initiatives that deliver meaningful business results.

What is a Social Media Presence?

When we talk about a social media presence, we are usually referring to the strategic use of the following platforms, which each have unique features for different digital audiences.

These four platforms were chosen based on relevance to the industry, with Instagram having the highest recommendation, due to its visual nature and the way audiences engage with content. For example, where do you look when you want to find the perfect new hair style, or balayage color? Instagram. For salons, Instagram is a mobile lookbook that acts in the same way as a digital business card.

Facebook is probably a close second due to its popularity with a complementary demographic. Instagram and Facebook are also integrated, so you can post the same content on both platforms. Pinterest would be recommended for similar reasons to Instagram but is less popular, and mostly used to communicate one way - meaning it does not have a community aspect. Lastly, TikTok, the newest member of the social media crew, has come in strong with video content that encompasses everything from tutorials to before and after looks.

Why Use Social Media?

  • Reach - how many people overall have viewed your post (fitness industry average is 3 million+)
  • Likes - how many people have engaged by ‘liking’ your post
  • Shares - how many people have shared your post on their own pages
  • Comments - how many people have engaged by commenting on your post
  • Tagging - how many people have tagged your company in a post of their own
  • Awareness - A user looks up #MidlengthHair on Instagram, sees your salon come up with engaging content, scrolls through your social presence and follows the account.
  • Findability – User continues to view content on social media and clicks through to website and reviews.
  • Reputation – User reads Google and Social reviews from salon customers and checks tagged photos (UGC).
  • Purchase – User books appointment online for your salon.
  • Experience – User posts a picture of their new hair and tags the salon/writes a review of the salon.

With 54% of browsers using social media to research products, this is often the first place people will interact with your brand. When executed well, social media platforms can act as a direct interface to your customers and target audiences and allow you to build long-lasting relationships. As the first port of call, most customers will look to social channels when they require support or customer service, making it key for relationship building and customer satisfaction.

Engaging Content

  • 79% of consumers aged 16 to 45 turn to online video to learn how to use a health or beauty product. So, there is a great opportunity here to create engaging content that not only helps consumers, but keeps your brand top of mind for future purchasing decisions. Tapping into this group of consumers involves being in the places they are and producing content that stands out from the crowd.

    As social platforms allow for a range of content, from video to polls to stories, you can capture your audience's attention in a variety of ways:

    Fitbit has built up over 705K followers by utilizing a range of content types that excite and delight their followers. They offer health tips, product updates, lifestyle videos, and wellness insights that are useful for their customer's everyday lives and inspire them to engage with the brand.

Community and Communication

  • One unique benefit of social media, that sets it apart from other marketing channels, is the ability to build an online community and user-generated content (UGC). This encourages conversation about your brand, as well as involving the consumer directly within the content creation process by allowing them to tag and share their own visuals of your company.

    Almost half (47%) of millennials and 36% of baby boomers trust UGC, whereas only 25% of both demographics say they trust branded content. This goes to show the importance of using social media in a way that encourages sharing real stories - the long-term impact of increased trust could be invaluable for your business.

    Athleta, the fitness brand, is a good example of creating an engaging social media presence that uses two-way communication. By sharing imagery of their customers (user-generated content) they are building an online community that showcases their products and vision.

Influence

  • Ultimately, social media is an invaluable tool for influencing your target customers with 74% of consumers relying on social networks to help with their purchasing decisions. Aim to educate, inspire, and entertain through content to achieve social media success.

    The main goal of marketing health and wellness on social media is more than just pushing sales, it’s about developing a loyal following and nurturing relationships to build advocacy.

Knowing Where to Focus

The best way to ensure you are picking the right channels for your brand is to ask yourself these questions:

  • Who is my target audience?
  • How do they use social media?
  • What is my brand vision and tone?
  • What are the industry trends?
  • What communications (outside of social media) are currently performing well?
  • What are competitors doing on social?

Have you ever bought the latest beauty, health, or wellness “must-have” purely because everyone else had it? Social media, when not used correctly, can feel a little bit like that. Everyone is on it, so brands want to be present on it too. But, without the proper direction and strategy, these brands end up just getting lost in the noise.

Even though we know that social media is a crucial tool in the marketing mix, you still need to be selective about which channels and platforms make the most sense for your company. Just like a good massage is tailored to benefit each person, your social media strategy needs to be created with your brand and target audience at the center.

If you know that your products or services are targeted towards a younger audience, you may focus on Instagram and TikTok instead of Facebook. Be true to your brand, and don’t be afraid to say no when it makes sense to do so.

Ensure Alignment

Nothing works well in isolation. Just like you wouldn’t apply only half a face mask, social media is just one part of your overall business and marketing strategy.

If your overall business objective is to grow brand awareness, then your social media should be focused on content that works towards this as well. Similarly, if your company is aiming to raise sales numbers, then the content should reflect this. Think about your audience and what actions you would like them to take.

Maya Chia Beauty does a good job of reflecting business goals within their social content. As a slightly lesser-known brand, they show their logo and branding a lot to help familiarize the audience with them, and they share a lot of product-based posts to educate and bring in a sales aspect.

Common threads should be obvious throughout your website, blog, email communications, PR, and social media. This ensures they are all working harmoniously together to deliver the best possible experience to your customers, as well as communicating your brand message effectively.

An example of this being executed well is The NOW massage boutique, they have a cohesive look and feel across their platforms, as well as a branded tone in their written communications.

Key Takeaways

There are a range of social media channels with different demographic and content options and it’s important to use them correctly to create a great connector between your brand and your target audience.

Since social media plays a crucial role in the consumer decision making process, take the time to make strategic decisions about which social media channels you want to invest in - you do not need to be everywhere (only where your customers are)! Finally, ensure you are aligning your social media objectives and content with wider business and marketing objectives to achieve the best results.
