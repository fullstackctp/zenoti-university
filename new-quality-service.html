Core Guest Satisfaction

Did you know that 90% of Americans say customer service is a factor in deciding whether to do business with a company?

That’s why delighting your guests is as important as the technical service that you provide. You can be the best at your craft, but if your business is not offering the best guest service, they may not consider you for their next appointment.

It is not just about making people look good, it’s about making people feel great.

Is your brand synonymous with making people feel great?

When your guests leave your center, you want them to walk out with confidence and to reflect positively on their experience. You want them to rate you 10 out of 10 and tell anyone who will listen that they must come to see you because you’re simply the best.

Let’s dive deeper into each aspect...

Service Quality

Let’s not forget why the guest has chosen to come to see you in the first place - to receive incredible service. Guests are looking for a well-executed service that incorporates all the right products and recommendations that are customized for them. A passion for education and sharing knowledge can make a big impact on a guest.

That is why consultations are one of the strongest tools that you have for your appointments. To properly prepare for the consultation, use their guest history, available in your Zenoti Employee Mobile App, to gain valuable insights into their goals, needs, and desired outcome.

When you do your homework prior to the consultation, you can uncover a lot about what the guest may need and create a personalized plan that is suitable for them, based on their routine and tendencies.

For example, “Your last haircut was ten weeks ago. I am going to recommend that you rebook eight weeks from today so we can prevent your ends from splitting”.

The consultation is the moment in the service where the guest's goals and your technical services align. A thorough consultation gives you the opportunity to clearly understand their intention for seeing you and gives you the opportunity to provide solutions to reach their desired result.

The consultation creates a roadmap for their service, one that you’re both aligned on. Without it, you are more likely to get lost along the way.

A thoughtful service provider is in tune with the cues of their guest. Pay attention to both verbal and nonverbal cues and if you feel that there is a disconnect, make an adjustment. You can also choose to include the guest throughout the service by asking questions.

For example, ask how they feel about the pressure of your massage, consult with them on the length before you take the first snip, ask them how they feel about a heavy highlight in their bangs. Including your guest shows them their look is a collaboration and you care about making sure that you hit the mark.

Once your technical service is complete, there’s one final step you need to take before your guest leaves the chair.

We call it the 3 Rs.

By recapping, recommending, and rebooking chairside, those elements become part of the service. Once the guest stands up, they are mentally “checked-out” and already thinking about their next venture. Employees can use their Employee Mobile App to add products to the guest shopping cart and to rebook for the next service.

This eliminates work for the front desk and adds an additional touch from the service provider to the guest.

Service providers should also encourage guests to download the Customer Mobile App, which gives them the ability to stay connected and to book or adjust appointments anywhere, at any time, from the convenience of their mobile device.

By educating the guest on convenient ways to engage with your salon you’re telling them, “You’re a valuable guest of mine, and I want you to have the right tools to make your experience seamless and easy.”

The guest should feel welcomed back by you and getting their appointment on the books is a great way to say, “I can’t wait to see you again”.

Personalization

“A person’s name is to him or her the sweetest and most important sound in any language” - Dale Carnegie.

People love to be recognized. It indicates respect and consideration. Something as simple as addressing a guest by their name can make a huge impact on their experience. So, when you greet a guest for the first time ask them how they would like to be addressed and save their preferences in their profile, ensuring that when they come to visit you everyone addresses them appropriately.

No two guests are the same and for that reason, no two appointments are the same.

Some guests come to you for peace and quiet, and an opportunity to relax. Others want to share their world with you and spend much of the appointment talking about themselves. You may have had the pleasure of providing a service to a guest on one of the best days of their lives, or the opportunity to provide comfort on one of the worst days. Customizing each appointment based on the guest’s needs will put you in sync with them and build trust.

The time with you becomes more than an appointment, you become their beauty professional.

You have addressed the guest by their name, and you have successfully read cues on how they want to interact with you. Now it is time to listen, really listen. Remembering tiny details about their lives that they have shared with you is a major WOW. And don’t worry, you are not expected to have the memory of an elephant. How is that achieved?

By being genuine in your recommendations based on what they need to achieve their goals. Personalizing the experience makes them feel like they are a VIP. It is an experience that they will share with their friends and one that they will be eager to have again as soon as possible. By personalizing their visit, and remembering small details of their life, you show them how much you value them as your guest.

Ambience

Whether the guest is in the waiting area, or in your chair they’re taking in what’s around them and picking up on the details of your space. What does your guest see when they look around?

Beyond visual cues, your guests are picking up on the vibe of your center using all their available senses. Every detail matters, from the music you play throughout the day, to the salon lighting, to the aromas and any refreshments you offer. It all matters, because these details are what paint a picture in the memories of your guests.

One additional way to check the ambience of your space is to assess your needs for repair and maintenance. Get your team members on board to report issues as they notice them. Guests may be forgiving to a broken piece of equipment at the time of their visit, but if they return six weeks later to see that it’s still broken, they will question the level of care that you put into your business.

For a spa guest, the center atmosphere is as important as ever. Your space should replicate the same serenity that you want your guest to experience. Plush robes, treatment table warmers, dimmed lighting and soft music are all small touches that can elevate the experience of relaxation for the guest. Encourage your staff to adapt to a “spa voice” when walking through your hallways so that relaxing guests are not disturbed.

Spa appointments may require intake forms or questionnaires prior to the start of the service. By utilizing digital forms, guests can fill out all necessary paperwork in advance from the convenience of their phone or computer, and the information that they provide will be stored in their guest profile. This enables them to relax when they come for their treatment, and eliminates the eyesore of clipboards, pens, and paper scattered about in an otherwise tranquil spa.

When considering ambience, it’s important to be mindful of all the marketing in your space. Allow your spa to be an escape from reality and sales pitches. The guest, at any point, does not want to feel inundated by your promotions and campaigns.

By utilizing digital marketing campaigns (text messages, emails, website marketing) you can clean up the marketing from your center. Additionally, any marketing you do have in your salon and spa will have more impact because it is not overcrowded. Highlighting too many promotions at once loses its effectiveness.

Pay attention to your retail shelves as well. Not only should they be clean and stocked, but they should be set up in a strategic manner. Keep your best-selling brands and products at eye level for an easy shopping experience.

You can have everything in tip-top shape, not a single towel out of place, but if your staff is not welcoming and friendly, your ambience won’t be either. Genuine smiles and warm greetings show a guest that you’re glad they’re there.

Small gestures, like complimenting the work of a co-worker, sweeping their station, or asking if there is anything that you can do to help them on a hectic day, are all examples of teamwork that a guest would like to see. It gives your guest a glimpse of a strong team culture that you worked hard to build. When a guest feels that the employees of a business are committed to the brand, it makes them want to commit in return.

Safety

We are all consumers, and we make choices every day about where we spend our time and money. Would you continue to visit a restaurant if you knew that the food makes you sick? Would you continue to use a product after it was recalled or drive a car if it didn’t pass a safety test? Probably not.

If your guests don’t feel safe when they come to your salon or spa, nothing else matters. Beauty providers are typically licensed professionals because safety is a priority.

In business, guest perception is your reality.

70% of a customer's journey is based on how they feel they’ve been treated.

Remember when we discussed core guest experience?

You can’t expect 10 out of 10 ratings if your guests don’t feel safe. If they feel that their safety is at risk when they’re in your care, they won’t be returning. And in fact, they’re more than likely sharing their experience with friends and family.

Your guest needs to feel confident that you have taken every necessary step to provide a sanitary, safe environment. What impression does a guest get when they look around your business?

Your guests need to know that sanitation is part of your process without you explicitly saying so.

Plus, imagine if they could walk in, enjoy your services, and walk out—no standing in line, no sharing devices, and no front desk interactions.
