Expand Your Customer Base and Drive Sales

Have you ever finished styling a guest’s hair only to be reminded of another guest who hasn’t been back for a while? Or sat down for a well-earned coffee and wondered what happened to this guest or that individual who promised they would return?

If so, you’re not alone.

All businesses experience customer attrition; the natural loss of a customer. And, unfortunately, retention rates in the beauty and wellness industry are low.

Yet globally the industry continues to grow year-over-year, and is expected to reach USD$217.25 billion by 2026. So, how can you ensure your business grows with the industry? By expanding your customer base.

Discover how...

Why is Expanding Your Customer Base Important?

  • To counter low retention rates.
    •

    • The retention rate for salons and spas in the U.S. currently sits at only 30%.

      This means, that if 100 guests visited your brand last month, 70 of those won’t return. While perhaps painful to hear, it’s important to understand guests are not guaranteed. They can be lost to the competition, employee turnover, prices, moving away, an unpleasant experience or many other factors.

  • To grow your business.
    •
    • Getting new clients is the lifeblood of any business, so if your aim is to grow then you need to grow your guest base. Even if you have the most loyal customers in the world who regularly rebook appointments and visit for treatments, you won’t see growth unless new guests are also coming in. Ultimately, guest count is a numbers game. The more guests you have, the more opportunities there are for them to invest in your services, products and brand - which equates to a growing profit.
  • To increase brand awareness.
    •
    • The more guests that experience what your brand offers, the more ambassadors you have in your corner. Word of mouth continues to be one of the leading sources of new guest acquisition. The more guests you have speaking fondly about your brand, the more your brand will be recognized, and the more appointments there will be in the book!

Max Depree said, “We cannot become what we want by remaining what we are.”

The same theory goes for your guest base. You cannot reach your goals by remaining content with the guests you once earned. Seek growth by tapping into guests that have yet to consider your business for their needs.

Let’s explore how.

Foundational Elements for Acquiring New Customers

You’ve invested dozens of hours and hundreds of dollars creating an ad campaign targeted at all the keywords your research showed you rank highly in search engines, but you’re not getting any results and your budget is quickly running out. So, what is it you’re doing wrong?

It could be that you’re not targeting the right audience!

Before implementing any customer acquisition strategy, it’s important to spend time gaining a full understanding of who exactly the individuals that you want to target are. This is what will help you stand out against the competition and catch the eye of the right guest that is looking to book their next salon or spa appointment.

When you have this awareness of the ideal customer, you can make the right decisions on factors such as messaging and media and ultimately achieve the best results.

Step 1: Understand your existing audience

In order to build an effective strategy to generate new customers, it’s important to first gain a comprehensive understanding of who your existing audience is and what drives them.

As a good starting point, it may help you to build out a few personas of your current customers and do some market research. Your providers know their guests inside and out, after all they spend all day interacting with them, so get the whole team involved and make it a collaborative, fun process.

Once you understand who your current customer is, what value you provide to them, and their unique needs, you can use this knowledge to build an effective strategy for attracting new customers.

Step 2: Understand your target audience

Having insights into your target audience enables you to prioritize the strategies and channels that will market most effectively to them. This is critical for ensuring you get the best possible return on your investment (ROI). It also means that you are using the right language and communication methods to establish and build relationships with them and can personalize these interactions to their needs for best impact.

1. Gift Cards

Today’s gift is tomorrow’s guest. And today’s gift is, of course, a gift card! Gift card sales are a wonderful indicator of future business and a crucial revenue-building tool.

How crucial?

  • In 2019, U.S. guests spent $190 billion dollars (about $580 per person) on gift card purchases.
  • 70% of all U.S. consumers claim to have bought a gift card as a present during a holiday season.
  • 65% of guests who receive a gift card spend 40% more than the card value.
  • Brands who offer Zenoti gift cards see over 180% jump in gift card revenue during holiday months.

Not only is this a significant amount of revenue to miss out on if you don’t sell gift cards, but research shows they actually influence the future of your brand too. This is because 11% of guests aged 18 to 34 are more likely to visit a brand they wouldn’t otherwise visit because they have received gift card.

Even more convenient?

Selling gift cards on your website! Rush Hair and Beauty now sell more than 50% of their gift cards online.

2. Marketing

Have you ever had a guest tell you, “Oh, I didn’t know you were located here” or “I had no idea you offered that!”?

If you have, you should look to improve your marketing.

Marketing is a tried-and-true way to grow your guest base. While it can be expensive, if you invest wisely the returns you see can far out way the initial costs.

This is because it makes your brand stand out from the crowd!

Marketing comes in both print and digital forms, with digital marketing growing increasingly each year. Now there are countless channels for you to use to reach your guests, such as websites, search engines, social media, email, and text, to name a few.

That’s all well and good but what types of things should you be marketing to new guests?

Your goal is to market what a guest wants. If there is a demand, you should be able to supply.

Let's take the COVID-19 pandemic as an example. This unique and unprecedented situation drastically increased global demand for safe, touchless technology solutions that enabled businesses to stay open and serving guests. The brands that were able to not only successfully implement these solutions but market them so that existing and potential new guests were aware of them, were those who ensured success through COVID.

By marketing a solution to a problem and highlighting what set them apart from competitors, in this case touchless technology, new guests opted for these service providers as a safe option for their needs.

3. Online Presence

It’s 10:00pm and you’re wide-awake thinking about all the things you need to get done this week. Suddenly, you realize you desperately need a pedicure. But you’ve not been able to find a regular spot that wows you.

What do you do?

That experience, from the moment you picked up your phone, was dictated completely by the brand’s online web presence. This online presence covers everything from your website, your guest mobile app, your engagement on social media, your communication channels, their accessibility, layout, imagery, and so much more.

Your online identity should be consistent with all your other branding in order to create a positive and consistent experience that evokes the type of reputation you hope to hold and the type of clientele that you want to attract.

Zenoti reports that 29% of first-time guests make their appointment using online booking & 50-80% of their appointments come from self-service booking solutions.

Research shows that not only are a lot of first-time guests booking their appointment online, 46% of appointments are being booked outside of typical business hours. This means that not having a strong online presence, which ensures guests find your brand and can then book an appointment once there, is losing you critical new guests. Plus, our customers are seeing higher average ticket prices from appointments that are booked online – revenue you certainly don’t want to miss out on!

Modern guests take their time when choosing which brand to visit. Accessing your website enables them to browse without feeling as if they are imposing on a receptionist. And the reality is, your front desk team is probably unable to spend ten minutes speaking with every guest that calls to book.

With this in mind, your website still needs to replicate the same extraordinary experience the guest would get when speaking with the front desk or entering your center. What does the guest see? Is there a flow? Is there an intuitive user experience? Is it clear where they should be going to access what they’re looking for? If your website is not guest centric, chances are they will not take the time to learn if your salon or spa is guest centric.

Finally, keep in mind that different generations have different opinions and behaviors when it comes to using the internet. Perhaps teenagers aren’t your target demographic currently, but they are your future business. So, remember that the services and programs that they are offered now will affect your ability to gain their loyalty.

4. Referrals

You've built a brand you are passionate about and that represents your values and beliefs. Therefore, it’s natural that your salon or spa would then attract an audience of like-minded individuals who love to visit because they are also aligned with your brand values and ethos.

What if there was an easy way to encourage other like-minded individuals to visit, since there’s a good chance they’ll also love what you offer?

Well, with a referral strategy you can do exactly this! All you need to do is establish a program that taps into your existing guests’ network of family and friends.

First things first, what are the benefits of guest referrals?

While there are basic ways to ask your clientele for referrals (hello, business cards!) there are more efficient ways to launch a lucrative referral program.

A successful referral program should:

  • Make it easy to refer.
    •

Offer multiple touchpoints for guests to choose from, such as WhatsApp, Email or Facebook

  • Target your loyal guests.
    •

Segment your guests based on spend, visits and frequency to identify those who are most loyal.

  • Be marketed across multiple channels.
    •

Create a across your store, mobile app, webstore, email campaigns, text messaging and social media.

  • Be prompted at the right time.
    •

Capitalize on the right moments to ask for a referral, such as after a positive review or experience.

  • Include double–sided incentives.
    •

Offer incentives to both the referrer and the referee

Implementing a referral program may be an easy solution for an expensive problem. If your guests are thinking about referring their friends and family, then give them a push to do so! It’s not just one extra guest you can expect to meet, it’s almost triple that since research shows that every referrer makes an average of 2.68 referrals. Don’t forget, the guests referred to your business will spend more in their lifetime of conducting business with you than guests who were not referred.

“Do what you do so well that they will want to see it again and bring their friends”  - Walt Disney

Key Takeaways

Ultimately, if you want to grow your brand, you must grow your network. While valuable, your existing guest base will only take you so far.

For every new guest you welcome, and rebook, you are building loyalty and growing your database of brand ambassadors. Committing to strategies like gift cards, marketing, establishing an online presence, and referrals will encourage new guests to visit your salon or spa. What you do once they’re there is up to you.
