Expand Your Customer Base and Drive Sales

Introduction

Have you ever finished styling a guest’s hair only to be reminded of another guest who hasn’t been back for a while? Or maybe, every time you sit down for a well-earned coffee break you start to wonder what happened to those guests who promised to return.

If so, you’re not alone.

All businesses experience customer attrition, which is the natural loss of customers. Unfortunately, attrition rates in the beauty and wellness industry run high. That’s why you need a defined strategy to gain—and retain—an ever-expanding base of customers!

What you’ll learn:

  • Why Expanding Your Customer Base is So Important
  • How to Build a Foundation for Acquiring New Customers
  • Four Strategies For Expanding Your Customer Base

Did you know?

  • Globally, the beauty and wellness industry is in a state of growth, and it’s expected to exceed $217B USD by 2026.
  • In the post-pandemic, vaccination-era, the rising demand for beauty and wellness solutions will propel consumers to find a trusted provider, making now the time to develop an acquisition plan.
  • The average guest retention rate for spas and salons in the U.S. is only 30%, leaving nearly two-thirds of the industry’s customer base as acquisition targets.

Why Expanding Your Customer Base is So Important

Max De Pree, author and business leader, said, “We cannot become what we need to be by remaining what we are.”

The same philosophy applies toward expanding your customer base. You cannot reach your business goals without a plan to consistently attract and acquire new clients. Even though it can cost 5X more to acquire a new client than to retain an existing one, you cannot remain content with your current guest count. A portion of your management efforts should always be focused on the tapping into guests that have yet to consider your business for their needs.

Here’s why expansion of your customer base is vital to your business.

Expanding Your Base Counters Low Retention Rates

With an average retention rate of 30%, it means even if you attracted 100 new clients in the last month, 70 of them won’t be returning for a follow up appointment. While that’s painful to hear, it’s important to understand guests are not guaranteed. They can be lost to the competition, employee turnover, prices, moving away, an unpleasant experience, and many other factors. Continually infusing new customers into your business will offset that attrition.

Acquiring New Clients Grows Your Business

Getting new clients is the lifeblood of any business, so if your aim is business growth, then you need to grow your guest base. Even if you have the most loyal customers in the world, you won’t see growth unless new guests are also coming in. Ultimately, guest count is a numbers game. The more guests you have, the more opportunities there are for them to invest in your services, products, and brand—which equates to a growing profit.

Brand Awareness Increases with Customer Growth

The more guests that experience what your brand offers, the more ambassadors you have promoting your business to others. Word of mouth continues to be a leading source of new guest acquisition for salons and spas. The more guests you have speaking fondly about your brand, the more your brand will be recognized, and the more appointments there will be on the books!

How to Build a Foundation for Acquiring New Customers

You’ve invested dozens of hours and hundreds (if not thousands) of dollars creating an ad campaign targeted at the keywords that rank highly for you on search engines, but you’re not getting any results and your budget is quickly running out. So, what are you doing wrong?

It could be that you’re not targeting the right audience!

Before implementing any customer acquisition strategy, it’s important to gain a full understanding of the exact individuals you want to target. This is will help your ads stand out against the competition and catch the attention of guests looking to book their next salon or spa appointment.

When you have this awareness of the ideal customer, you can make the right decisions on factors such as messaging and media, and ultimately achieve the best results. The following information will help you learn to target the right individuals.

Step 1: Understand Your Existing Audience

In order to build an effective strategy to generate new customers, it’s important to first gain a comprehensive understanding of who your existing audience is and what drives them.

As a good starting point, it may help to create a few customer personas of your current guests and do some market research on your industry and its competitors. Your providers know their guests inside and out, after all they spend each day interacting with them, so get the whole team involved and make it a collaborative, fun process.

While you are researching your current customer base, you will learn what they like about your brand and how they prefer to consume information about the brands they do business with. For example, they may turn to online resources like Facebook or Instagram, or they may particularly trust referrals and other word of mouth marketing.

Once you understand who your current customer is, what value you provide to them, and their unique needs, you can use this knowledge to build an effective strategy for attracting new customers.

1. Using Gift Cards

Today’s gift is tomorrow’s guest. And today’s gift is, of course, a gift card! Gift card sales are a wonderful indicator of future business and a crucial revenue-building tool.

How crucial?

  • In 2019, U.S. guests spent $190 billion (about $580 per person) on gift card purchases.
  • 70% of all U.S. consumers claim to have bought a gift card as a present during a holiday season.
  • 65% of guests who receive a gift card spend 40% more than the card value.
  • Brands who offer Zenoti gift cards see over 180% jump in gift card revenue during holiday months.

Not only is there a significant amount of revenue to miss out on if you don’t sell gift cards, but research shows they influence the future of your brand, too. That’s because 11% of guests aged 18 to 34 are more likely to visit a brand they wouldn’t otherwise visit because they have received gift card.

And don’t forget the convenience factor of selling your gift cards on your website! Online gift shopping is hotter than ever, and consumers now expect digital gift cards to be online at their favorite businesses. A salon that is doing it right is Rush Hair and Beauty. They now sell more than 50% of their gift cards online.

2. Create Marketing Campaigns

If you’ve ever had a guest tell you the following, it’s probably time to look at ways to improve your marketing.

“Oh, I didn’t know you were located here.” — OR— "I had no idea you offered that!”

Marketing is a tried-and-true way to grow your guest base and make your brand stand out from the crowd! While it can be expensive, if you invest wisely the returns you see can far out way the initial costs.

Marketing comes in both print and digital forms, with digital marketing growing increasingly each year. There are countless channels for you to use to reach your guests, such as websites, search engines, social media, email, and text, to name a few.

In addition to multiple marketing channels, there are multiple marketing tactics you can use to attract new guests.

Click each arrow below to reveal effective marketing tactics:

Share introductory pricing for first-time guests.

Create exclusive offers for those new to your salon or spa.

Promote BOGO and seasonal gift card offers.

Highlight your top-notch providers or brand differentiators.

Showcase your service menu and product assortment.

Emphasize your convenient location.

Show how you value your guests with perks and promotions.

Your goal is to market what a guest wants. If there is a demand, you should be able to supply. Let's take the COVID-19 pandemic as an example. This unique and unprecedented situation drastically increased global demand for safe, touchless technology solutions that enabled businesses to stay open and serving guests. The brands that were able to not only successfully implement these solutions, but also promote them so existing and potential guests were aware of them, were the businesses who ensured success through COVID.

By marketing a solution to a problem and highlighting what set them apart from competitors, in this case touchless technology, new guests opted for these service providers as a safe option for their needs.

3. Optimizing Your Online Presence

It’s 10:00pm and you’re wide-awake thinking about all the things you need to get done this week. Suddenly, you realize you desperately need a pedicure. But you’ve not been able to find a regular spot that wows you.

What do you do?

That experience, from the moment you picked up your phone, was dictated completely by the brand’s online presence.

An online presence covers everything from your website, your guest mobile app, your engagement on social media, your communication channels, their accessibility, layout, imagery, and so much more.

Your online identity should be consistent with all your other branding in order to create a positive and consistent experience that evokes the type of reputation you hope to hold and the type of clientele that you want to attract.

Zenoti reports that 29% of first-time guests make their appointment using online booking, and 50-80% of their appointments come from self-service booking solutions.

Research shows many first-time guests booking their appointment online, and 46% of appointments are booked outside of typical business hours. Having a strong online presence not only ensures potential guests can find your brand, it means they can also book an appointment for themselves—making it easy and convenient for them to try your business. Plus, Zenoti customers are seeing higher average ticket prices from appointments that are booked online – revenue you certainly don’t want to miss out on!

Modern guests take their time when choosing which brand to visit. Accessing your website enables them to browse without feeling as if they are imposing on a receptionist. And the reality is, your front desk team is probably unable to spend ten minutes speaking with every guest that calls to book.

With this in mind, your website still needs to replicate the same extraordinary experience the guest would get when speaking with the front desk or entering your center. Consider your website:

  • What does the guest see?
  • Is there an obvious flow?
  • Is the user experience intuitive?
  • Is the content clear and descriptive?
  • Can an online guest find what they are looking for?

If your website does not have a great guest experience, chances are they will not take the time to learn what their in-person experience will be.

Finally, keep in mind that different generations have different opinions and behaviors when it comes to using the internet. Perhaps teenagers aren’t your target demographic currently, but they are your future business. So, remember that the services and programs that are offered now will affect your ability to gain their loyalty.

4. Ramping Up Your Referrals

You've built a brand you are passionate about and that represents your values and beliefs. Therefore, it’s only natural your salon or spa would attract an audience of like-minded individuals who love to visit because they are also aligned with your brand values and ethos.

What if there was an easy way to encourage other like-minded individuals to visit, since there’s a good chance they’ll also love what you offer?

With a referral strategy you can do exactly that! All you need to do is establish a program that taps into your existing guests’ network of family and friends.

First things first, what are the benefits of guest referrals?

Click each arrow below to learn more.

54%

Word-of-mouth increases marketing efficiency by up to 54% (Wharton University).

4X

Referred guests are 4 times more likely to refer others to the same business (Wharton University).

16%

Guests referred by a friend or other guests have a 16% higher lifetime value than non-referred guests (SaaSquatch).

37%

Referred guests have a 37% higher guest retention rate than those who weren’t referred by someone (Think with Google).

65%

65% of all new business comes from referrals (The New York Times).

While there are basic ways to ask your clientele for referrals (hello, business cards!), there are more efficient and effective ways to launch a lucrative referral program. A successful referral program should include the following.

Click the (+) to learn more about each scenario.

Make It Easy to Refer

Offer multiple touchpoints for guests to choose from, such as WhatsApp, email, or Facebook.

Target Your Loyal Guests

Segment your guests based on spend, visits, and frequency to identify those who are most loyal.

Market Across Multiple Channels

Make use of marketing across your store, mobile app, webstore, email campaigns, text messaging, and social media.

Prompt for Referrals at the Right Time

Capitalize on the right moments to ask for a referral, such as after a positive review or experience.

Include Double-Sided Incentives

Offer incentives to both the referrer and the referee.

Implementing a referral program may be an easy solution for an expensive problem. If your guests are thinking about referring their friends and family, then give them a push to do so! It’s not just one extra guest you can expect to meet, it’s almost triple that since research shows that every referrer makes an average of 2.68 referrals. Don’t forget, the guests referred to your business will spend more in their lifetime of conducting business with you than guests who were not referred.

Key Takeaways:

Ultimately, if you want to grow your brand, you must grow your customer base. While valuable, your existing guest count will only take you so far.

For every new guest you welcome, and rebook, you are building loyalty and growing your database of brand ambassadors. Committing to strategies like gift cards, targeted marketing campaigns, optimizing an online presence, and ramping up referrals will encourage new guests to visit your salon or spa.
